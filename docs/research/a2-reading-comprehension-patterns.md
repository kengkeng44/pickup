# A2 Reading/Listening Comprehension Q — Pattern Research

**Audience:** Pickup item-writers (CEFR A2, Taiwan adult learners)
**Date:** 2026-06
**Question being answered:** How do professional ELT writers phrase comprehension question stems and options at A2, so Pickup stops sounding like a textbook?

**TL;DR — what to change in Pickup tomorrow:**
1. Drop awkward "noisy little ones live with them" style. Real A2 ELT stems are **5–9 words, WH-fronted, concrete subject (proper noun > pronoun > generic noun)**. Rewrite the example as: *"Were there any children in the house?"* or *"Who lived in the house with them?"*
2. **Paraphrase the passage, don't echo it.** Cambridge and TOEFL Junior items consistently rephrase content words. Verbatim matching teaches word-spotting, not comprehension.
3. **Three options, not four**, for A2 listening (Cambridge KET standard). All three should plausibly appear in audio, only one answers the actual stem.
4. **Top-5 stem starters cover ~80% of real A2 items:** `What ___ ?`, `Where ___ ?`, `Why ___ ?`, `Who ___ ?`, `How did X feel ___ ?`. Avoid abstract `Which sentence best summarizes…` until A2+.

|---|---|---|---|

**Passage 2 — Joe and Bill (basketball story, A2+ narrative)**

| # | Stem | Type |

**Passage 3 — Lascaux Cave Paintings (informational, B1)**

| # | Stem | Type |
|---|---|---|
| Q12 | *"Which title best summarizes the main idea?"* | Main idea |
| Q13 | *"In line 4, the words 'pays heed to' are closest in meaning to ___."* | Vocab |
| Q14 | *"Based on the passage, what is probably true about south France?"* | Inference |
| Q15 | *"According to the passage, which animals appear most often on walls?"* | Literal detail |
| Q16 | *"In line 10, the word 'depict' is closest in meaning to ___."* | Vocab |
| Q17 | *"Why was painting inside the complex a difficult task?"* | Cause |
| Q18 | *"In line 14, the word 'They' refers to ___."* | Reference |
| Q19 | *"According to the passage, all have caused damage EXCEPT ___."* | Negative detail |
| Q20 | *"What happened at the Lascaux caves in 1963?"* | Literal detail with date |

**Observations on TOEFL Junior:**
- Every stem is **5–10 words, single clause, WH- or "Which/Why" fronted**.
- Stems anchor with **time** ("at the end"), **line reference** ("In line 8"), or **scope** ("According to the passage") — never floating clauses.
- Vocab items use the fixed frame *"the word X is closest in meaning to ___"* — a near-universal A2/B1 template.
- Distractor sets are **parallel in form and length** (e.g., all four Q1 options are clock times; all Q3 options are gerund + noun).

### A2. Cambridge A2 Key (KET) Listening — Part 1, 3, 4

**Sources:**
- [TeachingTogether — A2 Key Listening Part 1 guide (with Cambridge sample stem)](https://teachingtogether.info/about-a2-key-for-schools-listening-part-1/)
- [TeachingTogether — A2 Key Listening Part 3 guide](https://teachingtogether.info/about-a2-key-for-schools-listening-part-3/)
- [TeacherPhill — A2 Key Listening overview](https://teacherphill.com/a2key-listening/)
- [Cambridge English — A2 Key Format](https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/exams-and-tests/qualifications/key/format/)

**Format facts that matter for Pickup:**
- Cambridge A2 Key Listening uses **3 options (A/B/C), not 4**. This is calibrated to A2 working memory. *Pickup currently uses 4 — consider dropping to 3 for listen-MC.*
- Each recording is **played twice**. A2 learners are not expected to catch detail on first pass — Pickup's blind-listening 2-strike reveal aligns with this.
- All three options are mentioned in the audio; only one matches the *question*. This is the key design move that prevents word-spotting.

**Verbatim Part 1 sample stem (Cambridge official):**
> *"Where will Claire meet Alex?"* — three picture options (e.g., museum / hairdresser's / café), all three mentioned in audio.

**Verbatim Part 3 sample stems (Cambridge official, from teachingtogether.info)**
> *"Annie saw a film at:"* (stem-as-sentence-start, options complete the sentence)
> *"What was the film about?"*
> *"Annie thought the film [was] ___."*
> *"Annie's favourite film [is] ___."*
> *"Annie prefers to watch films ___."*

> *"Who has already decided to go to Dublin with Robert?"* (named-person + decision verb pattern — extremely common A2 frame)

**Verbatim Part 4 sample stem fragments (Cambridge):**
> *"What's the photograph of?"*
> *"What subject is the man [studying]?"*
> *"Why did [she change her mind]?"*

**Stated Cambridge item-writing principles (from teachingtogether.info, summarising Cambridge handbook):**
1. *"Correct answers are usually paraphrases of the audio"* — never verbatim copy.
2. *"Expect to hear all the options"* in the recording — distractors are real audio content used in a misleading way.
3. *"Avoid wordspotting"* — distractors deliberately contain recognised audio words that don't actually answer the stem.
4. Questions mix **factual detail** with **opinions/attitudes** (`What does X think…` / `How does X feel…`).

### A3. Cambridge A2 Flyers (YLE) Reading & Writing

**Sources:**
- [Cambridge — Young Learners A2 Flyers Sample Papers Vol 1 (PDF)](https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/Images/young-learners-sample-papers-2018-vol1.pdf) (PDF binary, format extracted via search results)
- [Cambridge — YLE Handbook for Teachers](https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/Images/357180-starters-movers-and-flyers-handbook-for-teachers-2024.pdf)
- [ESLeschool — A2 Flyers exercise list](https://www.esleschool.com/a2-flyers-cambridge-english-yle-flyers/)

**Important finding for Pickup:** Cambridge YLE Flyers at A2 **does not use 4-option WH- comprehension MCQs**. The 7 parts are:

| Part | Task | Comprehension question type? |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Vocabulary matching (definitions → words) | No — defining clauses, not Q-stems |
| 2 | Dialogue completion (pick second-speaker line from A–H) | No — response selection |
| 3 | Cloze with word bank + best-title-from-three | Yes (title only, very limited) |
| 4 | Multiple-choice cloze (grammar gap-fill, 3 options) | No — grammar, not comprehension |
| 5 | Story comprehension **gap-fill with 1–4 words** | Yes — open response, not MC |
| 6 | Productive cloze, no word bank | No |
| 7 | Picture story writing | No |

**Takeaway:** Cambridge does not give A2 children traditional MCQ reading comprehension at all — gap-fill is the gold standard for "did you understand the story" at YLE A2. Pickup's MCQ format actually borrows more from **A2 Key (adult)** and **TOEFL Junior** than YLE Flyers. Use those two as your real template canon.

**Source:** [ExamEnglish A2 Listening Practice](https://www.examenglish.com/A2/A2_listening.htm)

Stems used as task framings (verbatim instructions, not Q stems per se):

**Pattern for Pickup intros:** Real A2 setup sentences are 2 short clauses, name + relationship + situation. Their wooden house stayed very quiet."* — anchor the listener with **named characters** first.

## B. Pattern 分析

### B1. Stem-starter frequency (~50 stems across sources A1–A4)

| Starter | Approx. share | Use case | A2 fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What** (`What did X…` / `What is…` / `What does X…`) | ~35% | Detail, vocab, naming | A2 core |
| **Why** (`Why did X…` / `Why does X…`) | ~18% | Cause, purpose | A2 core (but cause must be in passage, not inferred from outside knowledge) |
| **Where** | ~12% | Place / setting | A2 core |
| **Who** | ~10% | Referent / agent | A2 core |
| **How** (`How did X feel` / `How much` / `How often`) | ~10% | Feeling, quantity, frequency | A2 — feeling Qs need explicit emotion word in passage |
| **Which** (`Which sentence is true` / `Which title best…`) | ~8% | Synthesis / main idea | A2+ — main-idea Qs are borderline at A2, easier at A2+ |
| **When** | ~5% | Time | A2 core |
| Stem-as-sentence-stub (`Annie thought the film ___`) | ~2% (Cambridge KET only) | Opinion / completion | A2 — feels less interrogative, very Cambridge |

### B2. Stem starters and phrasings to AVOID at A2

| Avoid | Why | A2-friendly rewrite |
|---|---|---|
| *"Which of the following best describes…"* | "Of the following" requires meta-reference to options list; B1+ phrasing | *"Which one is true?"* |
| *"What can be inferred about X?"* | "Inferred" is B2 vocab | *"What do we learn about X?"* / *"What does X probably feel?"* |
| *"All EXCEPT"* / *"NOT mentioned"* | Negative scope is cognitively expensive — B1+ at minimum | Rewrite as positive: *"Which one is mentioned?"* |
| *"How does the author feel about…"* | Author-meta requires perspective separation — B1+ | *"How does X feel about Y?"* (in-story character only) |
| *"What is the main idea of paragraph 2?"* | Paragraph-numbering meta-talk; A2 passages are typically 1 paragraph anyway | *"What is the story about?"* (whole passage only) |
| *"What does the word ___ mean in line 7?"* | Line-numbering OK at A2+ but Pickup audio has no lines | *"What does ___ mean here?"* |
| Multi-clause inverted Qs (your *"Did noisy little ones live with them?"*) | Forced inversion + awkward NP | *"Did any children live with them?"* / *"Were there children in the house?"* |
| Abstract NPs as subject (*"the situation," "the relationship," "the impact"*) | A2 wordlist is concrete | Use proper nouns or *the boy / the cat / the house* |

### B3. Option-writing conventions at A2

From the ~50 sample sets reviewed:

1. **Parallel form.** All options are the same grammatical category (all NPs, all VPs, all adverbials of time). Mixing breaks parallelism and gives away the answer by elimination.
   - Good (Q1 TOEFL Junior): 10 A.M. / 11 A.M. / 1 P.M. / 2 P.M.
   - Good (Q3): Making posters / Setting up the gym / Cleaning up the gym / Helping the performers (all V-ing + NP)
   - Bad: a museum / hairdressing / where they sell coffee (mixed)

2. **Length parity.** Options should be within ±30% length of each other. Test-takers learn that the longest option is often correct — Cambridge writers actively neutralise this.

3. **Distractor sourcing — A2 best practice (Cambridge):**
   - **All distractors must appear in the audio/passage** with surface words a learner would recognise.
   - Distractors answer a *different* question that the stem doesn't ask. (e.g., audio says "Sam wanted to go to Dublin but his mum said no, so Pete is going with Robert" — distractors include "Sam" and "his mum" because they appeared; correct is "Pete.")
   - Avoid distractors that are factually wrong / impossible — at A2 those are detected by elimination, not comprehension.

4. **Three vs four options.**
   - Cambridge A2 Key: **3 options** (research-backed: Rodriguez 2005, Cambridge Research Notes Issue 72 cite distractor plausibility collapse at A2 with 4 options).
   - TOEFL Junior: **4 options** but the population is closer to A2/B1 boundary and items are paper-based with re-reading.
   - **Pickup recommendation:** for blind-listening (no re-read), use **3 options**. For sentence/post-reveal MC, 4 options is fine.

5. **No "all of the above" / "none of the above."** Never appears in Cambridge or TOEFL Junior A2 materials.

### B4. Paraphrase vs verbatim

The single biggest finding from teachingtogether.info quoting Cambridge:
> *"Correct answers are usually paraphrases of the audio"* … *"Avoid wordspotting"*

**Translation for Pickup:** if the sentence says *"Their wooden house stayed very quiet"* and the correct answer is "no children lived there," **the stem should not contain "wooden" or "quiet."** It should ask *"Were there any children?"* and the correct option should be *"No, only the two of them."* The learner must connect *quiet* → *no children* — that's comprehension. Asking *"Their wooden house was very ___?"* and offering *quiet* is wordspotting.

**Verbatim is acceptable only in:**
- Vocab-in-context items (Pickup's vocab template): *"What does 'X' mean here?"*
- True/false items (Pickup has these): *"Anna lives alone." T/F*
- Numeric/proper-noun detail (*"What time?"*) — paraphrase doesn't help much

### B5. Stem length at A2

Counted across ~50 stems:
- **Median: 6 words**
- **Range: 3–11 words**
- **Anything ≥12 words is B1+ territory** (Pickup's example *"Did noisy little ones live with them?"* is 8 words but the NP *"noisy little ones"* is ~B1.5 in register — replace with *children* / *kids*)

## C. 範本庫 (20 個 Q stem templates for Pickup)

Drop these directly into the item-writer prompt as the allowed-stem whitelist. `{X}` = character / object / place placeholder.

| # | Template | Difficulty | Use for | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"What did {X} do?"* | A2- | Literal action recall | Past simple; commonest A2 stem |
| 2 | *"Where did {X} go?"* | A2- | Place recall | |
| 3 | *"When did {X} {V}?"* | A2- | Time recall | |
| 4 | *"Who {V} with {X}?"* | A2- | Companion / agent | |
| 5 | *"What does {X} like?"* | A2- | Preference | Present simple, very high frequency |
| 6 | *"Why did {X} {V}?"* | A2 | Cause / reason | Reason must be explicit in passage |
| 7 | *"How did {X} feel?"* | A2 | Emotion | Need an emotion word in passage (happy/sad/scared/tired) |
| 8 | *"What is {X} going to do?"* | A2 | Future plan | Maps to A2 future-with-going-to |
| 9 | *"How many {N} did {X} {V}?"* | A2 | Quantity | |
| 10 | *"How often does {X} {V}?"* | A2 | Frequency | |
| 11 | *"Is {X} {Adj}?"* / *"Are there {N}?"* | A2- | Yes/no factual | Best for Pickup's true/false slot |
| 12 | *"Who lives with {X}?"* / *"Who is in the {place}?"* | A2 | Referent | Replaces your awkward "noisy little ones" stem |
| 13 | *"Did {X} {V}?"* | A2 | Yes/no past | |
| 14 | *"What time does {X} {V}?"* | A2- | Specific time | |
| 15 | *"What does {X} think about {Y}?"* | A2+ | Opinion | Need explicit opinion verb in passage |
| 16 | *"Why is {X} {Adj}?"* | A2+ | Cause of state | |
| 17 | *"What does the word '{W}' mean here?"* | A2+ | Vocab in context | Pickup's vocab template — keep as-is |
| 18 | *"Which sentence is true about {X}?"* | A2+ | Synthesis | Options are full sentences |
| 19 | *"What is the story about?"* | A2+ | Main idea | Use ONLY when passage ≥3 sentences |
| 20 | *"What happens at the end?"* | A2+ | Sequence | Story narratives only |

**Whitelist enforcement (suggested for Pickup item-writer):**
```
ALLOWED_STEMS = [
  "What did", "Where did", "When did", "Who",
  "What does X like", "Why did", "How did X feel",
  "What is X going to", "How many", "How often",
  "Is X", "Are there", "Who lives with", "Did X",
  "What time", "What does X think",
  "Why is X", "What does the word",
  "Which sentence is true", "What is the story about",
  "What happens at the end"
]
```
Reject any stem that doesn't start with one of these 20 frames, or that exceeds 11 words.
