## 1. Story Arc (poetic narration, no dialogue)

Grandma turns the page. The fire is low. Tonight, she says, is a quiet tale.

A king had six bright sons and one small daughter. He took a second wife who hid a witch's heart. One morning the queen found the sons in the forest and threw six white shirts upon them. Feathers grew. Wings opened. Six swans rose into the cold sky and were gone.

The sister, alone in the great house, would not stay. She walked into the forest until her feet bled. At dusk she found a hut with seven small beds. The swans came home at sundown, became boys for a quarter hour, and told her how to break the spell: she must sew six shirts from starflowers, and for six years she must not speak, and she must not laugh. One word, one laugh, and the magic would tear.

So she gathered. Starflowers in the field, starflowers by the river, starflowers under thorns that bit her hands. She wove. She did not speak. She did not laugh.

A young king found her in a tree and took her home. He loved her silent face. His mother did not. The mother whispered against her until the king believed. They built a fire to burn her.

The last shirt was not yet finished. One sleeve was missing. They led her to the flames. She held the shirts to her chest. Six swans came down through the smoke. She threw the shirts. Six brothers stood beside her. The youngest had a swan's wing in place of one arm, where the sleeve had not been done. She opened her mouth and at last she spoke. The fire was put out. The mother was sent away. The brothers were brothers again.

Outside the yard, Mochi blinks slowly. Hana sighs. Grandma closes the book. The night is still.
