# Chapter 6 Spec — Baba Yaga 雞腳屋

> 拾光 (Pickup) Ch6 content design · Russian folktale (Vasilisa the Brave) · A2 Taiwanese learners
> Outer-frame: Mochi (stray cat) visits Grandma's yard nightly · Hana at side · Grandma reads
> Aesop sides: 漁夫與妻子 (The Fisherman and His Wife) + 七張床 (Goldilocks-style "Seven Beds")

---

## 1. Story arc (250 words)

**Outer frame.** Tonight feels colder than the others. Mochi jumps onto the low wall and senses the air is heavier. Hana is already curled at Grandma's feet. Grandma opens a thick book with a dark cover and warns, "This one is from far away. It is a little scary, but the brave girl wins."

**Main story — Vasilisa and Baba Yaga.** A girl named Vasilisa lives with a cold stepmother. Her real mother is gone, but she left a tiny doll. The doll is magic: it eats a little bread, then it helps. One night the stepmother says, "Go to the deep woods. Ask old Baba Yaga for fire. We have none." The path is long. Trees lean close. Vasilisa walks for hours. She finds a strange house. The house stands on two big chicken legs. It can turn around. Inside lives Baba Yaga, an old witch with iron teeth. The witch says, "Do my work, or I eat you." She gives hard tasks: clean the yard, sort the seeds, cook the soup. Vasilisa is tired and afraid, but she feeds the doll. The doll works in the dark while she sleeps. Every morning the tasks are done. Baba Yaga is angry but must keep her word. She gives Vasilisa a glowing skull on a stick — fire to take home. Vasilisa walks back through the woods. The skull lights the path. At home, the cold stepmother is gone forever. Vasilisa is safe.

**Outer outro.** Mochi shivers a little. Hana presses closer to Grandma. Grandma smiles softly: "Brave girls go home. So do brave cats."

## 5. TOEIC parity audit

| Type | Ch1 count (24 lessons) | Ch6 plan (25 lessons) | Parity notes |
|------|------------------------|------------------------|--------------|
| `listen-mc` (4-choice short audio) | ~38 | ~38 | TOEIC Part 1/2 analogue · 1+1+1+1 trap distractor enforced |
| `listen-comprehension` (Wh-Q inference) | ~22 | ~24 | TOEIC Part 3 analogue · answer must be **inferred**, not literal — e.g. L4-q6 "treats her **not kindly**" comes from "very cold to her" without "kind/unkind" appearing in the sentence |
| `type-what-you-hear` (free type w/ 4 hint tiles) | ~22 | ~24 | TOEIC Part 1 dictation analogue · phonetic confusables in distractors (jump/jog/join/joke, scary/starry/shiny/sleepy, pocket/packet/pond/porch) |

| `listen-emoji` (feeling/state pick) | ~14 | ~15 | No direct TOEIC analogue (Pickup-specific affect anchor) · 1 correct + 3 plausible-mood traps |
| `tap-pairs` (review only) | 3 (l24) | 3 (l25) | Review lesson identical to Ch1 |
