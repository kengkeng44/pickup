## 1. Story Arc (250 words)

Tonight is the **last evening** Mochi will climb Grandma's wall this season. The wind is dry and Grandma opens an old book bound in red silk. "This story is older than Cinderella," she tells Hana. "It is from 唐朝, more than a thousand years ago."

Inside the story: **Ye Xian (葉限)** is a quiet girl who lives with her cruel stepmother and stepsister after her father dies. Her only friend is a **golden-eyed fish** in the village pond. She feeds it crumbs from her bowl and whispers to it every evening. The fish grows large and shines under the moon. The stepmother discovers the fish, **kills it**, and cooks it.

Ye Xian weeps by the empty water. An old man with white hair appears: "Bury the bones. They will hear your wishes." She does. The **fish bones** give her a 金 robe, a 紅 cloak, and tiny **golden slippers** when the cave festival comes. She dances among lanterns; the **king** of a nearby kingdom sees her and follows. She runs — a slipper drops. The king searches every village for the foot that fits. He finds her. They marry. The stepmother is buried under stones; the bones rest quiet.

Outside the story, Hana asks: *"Was Ye Xian scared?"* Mochi looks at the moon and says: *"Yes. But she still went to the festival."* Grandma closes the book. "That's the end of our eight nights, little ones." Mochi jumps off the wall slowly tonight. The yard smells like jasmine. She does not know if she will come back tomorrow — but every story is now inside her, small as a fish bone, bright as a slipper.

### Trap Distractor Audit Pattern (mandatory 1+1+1+1)

For every 4-option Q, distractors must be:
- **Slot A**: correct answer
- **Slot B**: phonetic/spelling near-miss (e.g. `stray` vs `stay`)
- **Slot C**: semantic decoy in same domain (e.g. `stray` vs `wild`)
- **Slot D**: morphological/grammar trap (wrong tense / wrong POS / wrong number)
- **Slot E** (4th): unrelated content distractor (different domain)

If a Q has 4 options, pick 1 correct + 3 from {B, C, D, E}. **Never** 3 phonetic decoys (low pedagogical value).

**Audit findings (sample)**:
- L1-Q1 `["the last night", "the first night", "the longest night", "the loudest night"]` — Correct, B (loudest=phonetic near-miss "last"), C (first=semantic antonym), D (longest=morphological -est trap). Passes.
- L4-Q2 `["he died", "he left for work", "he is sleeping", "he moved away"]` — Correct + 3 semantic euphemism decoys. Passes (semantic depth, not phonetic).
- L6-Q1 pond options — pond/road/wall/cave — Correct + 3 semantic place-decoys. Passes.
- L15-Q* (slipper-drop): future audit — ensure distractors include morphological trap (e.g. "slip / slipped / slipping / slipper").

## 7. Special Finale Element — Epilogue Lesson 25 (Graduation Moment)

> **The closing button on the entire v2.0 8-chapter arc.** L25 is intentionally **not a normal review-pairs**. It's a hybrid: tap-pairs across all 8 chapters (the "graduation diploma") + a final Mochi reflection question. This is the player's commencement.

### Design Goals
1. **Cross-chapter vocab consolidation** — 1 tap-pairs Q with 4 words drawn from Ch1-Ch8 (one per chapter would be 8 — too many; pick **4 finale-relevant ones** + reveal the other 4 in explanationZh as a "diploma roll-call").
2. **Emotional closure** — 4 short reflection Q (one per 2 chapters: Ch1-2, Ch3-4, Ch5-6, Ch7-8) where Mochi names the moment she remembers most.
3. **Player agency at the end** — the very last Q is open-ended-style (still A2 4-choice but framed as "which story did you keep?") — no wrong answer pedagogically; we set `correctIndex: 0` but all 4 are emotionally valid (this is documented as a content lever).
4. **Re-engagement hook** — final explanationZh hints that Mochi might come back ("but tomorrow is also a night...") — leaves the door open for Ch9+ without contradicting the finale.

> Lonely fisherman picks up a big 田螺, brings it home. Next morning his rice is cooked and floor is swept. He hides and watches: a girl steps out of the shell to cook. Moral: kindness returns when not asked.

- L19 beats: fisherman picks up snail, brings home, leaves it in jar.
- L20 beats: morning rice cooked, fisherman hides, girl emerges from shell.
- Vocab focus: snail, fisherman, shell, cook, kind, hidden.

### L21-L22 — 嫦娥奔月 (Chang'e Flies to the Moon)

> Houyi shoots down 9 suns, earns elixir from the gods. His wife Chang'e drinks it to keep it safe from a thief and floats up to the moon. She lives there alone with a 玉兔 (jade rabbit). Moral: love and separation; the moon is her loneliness.

- L21 beats: Houyi shoots suns, asks gods for medicine, brings home.
- L22 beats: thief breaks in, Chang'e drinks elixir, floats to moon, sad and bright.
- Vocab focus: moon, sun, fly, drink, alone, rabbit (jade rabbit), sky.

Both aesops have **direct thematic resonance with the finale**: 田螺 = secret kindness in a lonely home (mirrors fish + Ye Xian); 嫦娥 = the moon that shines over both Ye Xian's pond and Mochi's wall. Recommended sequencing: L19-L20 between main-story L11 (weeping) and L12 (old man) — gives players a softer break before fish-bone magic. L21-L22 between main-story L18 (marriage) and outro L23 — moon imagery bridges into Goodnight.

> **Actual ordering decision**: Section 2 keeps Aesops L19-22 *after* main-story L18 for narrative cleanliness. Engineering may choose to interleave at build time via lesson re-ordering if UX testing shows pacing drag. Both orderings are valid.
