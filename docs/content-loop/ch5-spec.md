## 1. Story Arc (Kipling tone, ~250 words)

Now this, O Best Beloved, is a tale from the time when the world was new-and-all, and the Animals were just beginning to work for Man. The Horse had his saddle, and the Dog had his collar, and the Ox had his yoke — but in the middle of a Howling Desert there lived a Camel, and the Camel did not want to work. Not one little bit.

When the Horse came to him on Monday morning and said, "Camel, O Camel, come and trot like the rest of us," the Camel only said, "Humph!" and no more.

When the Dog came on Tuesday with a stick in his mouth and said, "Camel, O Camel, come and fetch and carry like the rest of us," the Camel only said, "Humph!" and no more.

When the Ox came on Wednesday with the yoke on his neck and said, "Camel, O Camel, come and plough like the rest of us," the Camel only said, "Humph!" and no more.

At the end of the day the Three came to the Djinn of All Deserts — the magic person who is in charge of all the deserts — and they told him about the lazy Camel. The Djinn rolled himself up in a cloud of dust and went across the desert, and he found the Camel looking at his own reflection in a pool of water, doing nothing at all.

"My long and bubbling friend," said the Djinn, "what is this I hear of your doing no work?"

"Humph!" said the Camel.

The Djinn sat down with his chin in his hand and began to think a Great Magic. And as he thought, the Camel's lovely flat back began to swell, and swell, and swell — and lo and behold, there grew up a great big lolloping Humph upon it.

"That Humph," said the Djinn, "is your very own, for being so idle. From this day on you will work three days without eating, because your Humph will feed you. And do not say I never did anything for you. Now come out of the desert and join the Three, and behave."

And from that day to this, O Best Beloved, the Camel always wears a humph (which we now call a hump, to be polite); but he has never yet caught up with the three days he missed at the beginning of the world. And he has never yet learned how to behave.

## Appendix A — Aesop Side Story Sketches (L19-L22)

### 蘆葦與橡樹 (The Reed and the Oak) — L19 + L20

**Beat 1 (L19)**:橡樹笑蘆葦軟弱,風一吹就彎。蘆葦不回嘴。
**Beat 2 (L20)**:大暴風來,橡樹硬撐被連根拔起,蘆葦彎腰活下來。
**Moral**:強硬不如柔軟 (yielding beats stiffness)。
**Vocab focus**:reed, oak, wind, bend, break, strong.

### 老鼠開會 (Belling the Cat) — L21 + L22

**Beat 1 (L21)**:老鼠們開會,有隻小鼠提議在貓脖子掛鈴,這樣聽到聲音就能逃。
**Beat 2 (L22)**:大家都拍手叫好,可是沒有任何老鼠敢去掛。
**Moral**:好點子要有人能做才算數 (an idea is not a plan)。
**Vocab focus**:mouse, meet, bell, idea, brave, none.

## Appendix B — Production Notes

1. **Kipling voice carve-out**:Inner-story 用 2nd person ("O Best Beloved", "you will see"),outer frame 維持 Mochi 1st person ("I sit", "I feel")。tag `kipling-voice` 標出特殊韻律句,供未來 TTS 調 rate / pause。
2. **{catName} / {dogName} 必須是 placeholder**:本 spec 所有句子都用 `{catName}` / `{dogName}`,runtime `applyCatName()` / `applyDogName()` 注入。直接寫 Mochi / Hana 會破壞 v2.0.A.8 玩家改名機制。
3. **L7 / L11 / L16 "Humph!" 重複**:刻意設計三次拒絕為記憶錨,SRS 注入時 `humph` 已自然曝光 3 次。
4. **Distractor 池來源**:phonetic / spell / topical 干擾從 WordHint dictionary 與 Ch1-4 已用過的詞抽,避免新字干擾 (符合 A2 cognitive load)。
5. **驗收**:本 spec 寫成後,下一步用 `tools/validate-lessons.js` 把 L1-L25 全部 JSON 化,過 LessonsSchema parse。
6. **Q 數差異**:本章 25 lessons + 1 bonus = 26 entries,Q 總數 150 (140 regular + 10 bonus)。對 Ch1 的 24 lessons / ~110 Q,Ch5 多 +1 lesson +40 Q,因 main-story 從 ~12 lessons × 5 Q 升 15 lessons × 6 Q 反映 Kipling 故事密度。建議 Plan 5 ship 前 review 是否要拉齊到 24 lessons 標準。
