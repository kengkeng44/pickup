## A. Angle research — MemBot pattern (5 bullets)

- **Chat-bubble thread UI**: MemBot renders messages as WhatsApp-style left/right bubbles. Each AI message shows a 40px round avatar + character name. Scrollable history means past conversation is always recoverable with clear speaker identity on every line.
- **Speaker-mapped avatars per turn**: Grammar Buddy, Role Play Buddy, Translator Buddy all have distinct illustrated avatars. Switching persona changes the entire visual anchor — users never confuse who is speaking mid-session.
- **"Typing..." affordance**: When MemBot is "thinking" between user input and the next AI message, a 3-dot pulsing indicator shows. Removes perceived blank-state anxiety; creates anticipation instead.
- **Character-voiced feedback**: Correct/wrong feedback is delivered as a character speech bubble ("Nice, I love that!", "Hmm, not quite — I think you mean...") rather than a color-coded banner. The character's voice stays consistent with its persona through praise/correction.
- **Session recap in character voice**: At session end, MemBot writes a 1-2 sentence "what we practiced today" summary in its own voice before showing stats. Reinforces story continuity and deepens persona attachment.

## B. Pickup 現狀 (對齊 MemBot angle)

- **SpeakerBadge exists but is 11px pill, not avatar**: `renderers.tsx:141-155` renders a small colored badge (🐱 Mochi / 👵 Grandma / 🐕 Hana / 📖 Narrator). No round avatar image — identity signal is weak vs. Memrise's 40px character photo on every bubble.
- **All narration bubbles show calico-anchor.webp regardless of speaker**: `NarrationRenderer` line 185 hardcodes `<img src="/mascots/calico-anchor.webp" width={44} height={44} />`. When `q.speaker === 'grandma'`, the bubble still shows Mochi's cat face — visual identity is wrong for the active speaker.
- **NarrativeLine history is speaker-anonymous**: `LessonPage.tsx:134-149` — scrolled history lines show a generic 22px speaker icon. No `SpeakerBadge`, no avatar. User reading back through scroll cannot tell if a line was Grandma, Mochi, or narrator. Memrise's chat thread always shows the speaker on each line.
- **No "typing" affordance between question renders**: Question transitions are instant state swaps (`setIdx(i => i + 1)`). On slower devices or TTS load, there is a brief blank moment. No 3-dot indicator — blank state feels broken rather than building anticipation.
- **CompletePanel has no character-voiced story recap**: `LessonPage.tsx:250-386` — CompletePanel opens with confetti + stats triptych (XP/ACCURACY/TIME). There is no Grandma or Mochi text recapping what story was heard. Memrise's session-end summary is the warmth payoff; Pickup skips it entirely.

## D. Top 3 actionable (S effort)

1. **P0 — Speaker-mapped avatar in NarrationRenderer** (`renderers.tsx:185`)
   - Add `const SPEAKER_AVATAR: Record<string, string> = { mochi: '/mascots/calico-anchor.webp', grandma: '/mascots/grandma-avatar.webp', hana: '/mascots/hana-avatar.webp', narrator: '/mascots/calico-anchor.webp' };`
   - Replace hardcoded `calico-anchor.webp` with `SPEAKER_AVATAR[q.speaker ?? 'narrator'] ?? SPEAKER_AVATAR.narrator`
   - If grandma/hana asset missing, use a 36px emoji fallback div instead of `<img>` — same size, no 404
   - **Impact**: Grandma's lines finally show Grandma visually. Core brand coherence for the "奶奶睡前故事" positioning.

2. **P0 — NarrativeLine speaker identity in history** (`LessonPage.tsx:92-94, 134-149`)
   - `onAdvance(snapshot)` call sites: prefix snapshot with speaker tag, e.g. `onAdvance(q.speaker ? `${q.speaker}::${text}` : text)`
   - `NarrativeLine` parses prefix → renders a 20px speaker emoji badge left of text
   - **Impact**: Scroll-up history becomes a named-character transcript, reinforcing the story world.

3. **P1 — CompletePanel story recap in Grandma's voice** (`LessonPage.tsx:~267`)
   - In `CompletePanel`, derive the first `type=narration` sentence from `lesson.questions`
   - Render it as a small amber-border blockquote with `SpeakerBadge speaker="grandma"` above the stats triptych
   - **Impact**: Lesson close feels like a warm story callback ("奶奶說了什麼...") instead of a cold scorecard — directly mirrors MemBot's session-end character-voice recap.
