## A. Angle research — Anki SRS card design patterns (5 bullets)

- **Front/back card state machine**: clean pre-reveal (question only, zero noise) → tap "Show Answer" → full back reveal + 4 rating buttons (Again / Hard / Good / Easy). Rating directly feeds SM-2 interval multiplier; users can *override* the algorithm's judgment on every card.
- **Due-count surface as primary CTA**: Anki home screen centers the "X cards due today" number. The deck list shows New / Learning / Review counts per deck before the user even taps in. Obligation is visible, which drives habit loop (see each other day target).
- **Card type labeling**: each card shows type badge (New / Learning / Review) so learners know whether they're seeing something for the first time or returning to a forgotten item. "Young" vs "Mature" cards also color-coded in stats.
- **Stats heatmap + forecast graph**: after any session, Anki shows a calendar heatmap (review history) + a bar chart of future due counts (next 30 days). Learners can *see* the forgetting curve being fought — transforms abstract retention into visible evidence.
- **Lapse handling**: if a card in "Review" state is answered Again, it regresses to "Relearning" with a shortened interval. Ease factor drops permanently (ease hell problem). This prevents premature graduation and models human forgetting accurately.

## B. Pickup 現狀 — aligned to Anki angle (5 bullets)

- **SRS wiring is entirely broken in v2.0**: `LessonPage.tsx` and `renderers.tsx` have **zero calls to `addToSrs()` or `removeFromSrs()`**. Wrong answers fire `onAnswer(i, false)` → PostHog only. The SRS queue in `storyKitten.ts` is never populated from any v2.0 lesson. Master matrix item B rank-4 ("完整 SRS") understates urgency — the *current* SRS is silently dead.
- **SRS reviews never surface in v2.0**: `srsReviewBatch()` is only called from `runStore.startRound()` which powers the legacy Phaser `PlayScene`. The new `LessonPage` never queries the review queue. Even if the queue had items from legacy sessions, they would never appear.
- **No due-count surface**: nowhere in MapPage, TasksPage, HUD, or BottomNav does the player see "N items to review today". Anki's home screen makes this the single largest number on screen. Pickup has XP / coins / streak in HUD but zero retention-loop signal.
- **Binary correct/wrong, no self-rating**: after revealing an answer, Pickup auto-advances after 3 s (correct) or 4 s (wrong) with no player input on difficulty. Anki's 4-button rating (Again/Hard/Good/Easy) is what makes SRS personal — Pickup removes this agency entirely.
- **No card-type / review-stage labeling**: player cannot distinguish a "new" question from an SRS "review" question. In Anki this is visually differentiated (badge + different deck counters). In Pickup there is no surface for this, making reviews feel identical to first encounters.
