# Canon — Archimedes' Eureka Moment (阿基米德 — 浴缸裡發現的秘密)

> Source: Classical Greek anecdote about Archimedes of Syracuse (287-212 BCE). First recorded by Vitruvius in *De architectura* Book IX (c. 25 BCE), ~200 years after Archimedes lived. Public domain worldwide — Vitruvius died c. 15 BCE, the anecdote is >2200 years old, and the underlying historical figures (Archimedes, King Hiero II of Syracuse) are antiquity.
> **NOT used**: any modern children's book / TV / animation / film adaptation. No specific named translator's later additions retained.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch26. Vitruvius plot beats only; A2 self-paraphrased sentences (no specific translation copied).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The King's Worry
- Long ago in the city of Syracuse, there lived a clever man named Archimedes.
- The king had a new crown of bright gold.
- But the king worried that some silver was mixed in with the gold.
- He sent for Archimedes and asked him to find out the truth.

**Vocab**: king / crown / gold / pure / clever

### Beat 2 · The Puzzle
- Archimedes held the crown in his hands and looked at it for a long time.
- He could not cut it open. He could not melt it down.
- "How can I tell what is inside, without breaking it?" he thought.
- He went home with the puzzle big in his head.

**Vocab**: hold / cut / break / inside / puzzle

### Beat 3 · Days of Thinking
- For days Archimedes sat at his desk and drew shapes on the floor.
- He did not eat much. He slept very little.
- His friends grew worried about him.
- At last, he decided to take a warm bath to rest his mind.

**Vocab**: think / desk / day / tired / bath

### Beat 4 · Into the Tub
- The bath was full to the top with warm water.
- Archimedes stepped in slowly, one foot at a time.
- As he sat down, water spilled over the edge onto the floor.
- He looked at the water on the floor with wide, bright eyes.

**Vocab**: tub / water / step / spill / edge

### Beat 5 · The Idea
- "The water that came out is the same size as my body in the tub!"
- Now he knew. Gold and silver have different sizes for the same weight.
- He could put the crown in water and watch the water rise.
- He jumped out of the bath and called out, "Eureka! Eureka!"

**Vocab**: idea / size / weight / rise / shout

### Beat 6 · Telling the King
- Archimedes ran to the palace with a big smile.
- He told the king his plan, and the king brought a bowl of water.
- They put the crown in. They put gold of the same weight in.
- The two pulled out different amounts of water — the crown was not pure gold.

**Vocab**: run / palace / plan / bowl / amount

### Beat 7 · A Gift to Science
- The king thanked Archimedes for finding the truth.
- The rule from that bath became part of science forever.
- We still call it Archimedes' law today.
- And every kid who tries an idea in the bath is a little like him.

**Vocab**: thank / science / law / today / try
