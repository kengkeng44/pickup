# Canon — Yexian (葉限)

> Source: Tang dynasty *Youyang Zazu* by Duan Chengshi (~860 CE). World's earliest known Cinderella-type tale, ~700 years before Perrault.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch7, bilingual zh-en code-switch (primary EN, occasional ZH highlight).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — A Girl in the Cave Village
- Long ago in southern China, a girl named Yexian (葉限) lived in a cave village.
- Her mother died when she was small. Her father died too.
- The new wife, 後母 (step-mother), took her in.
- The new wife had her own daughter. She gave Yexian all the hard work.

**Vocab**: village / die / wife / own / hard

### Beat 2 · The Red-Fin Fish
- Yexian's only friend was a fish in the pond.
- It had red fins (紅鰭) and golden eyes. It grew big and tame.
- Every day Yexian gave it food. The fish came up to her hand.
- The step-mother saw this. She felt cold inside.

**Vocab**: friend / pond / fin / tame / cold

### Beat 3 · The Trick
- The step-mother put on Yexian's torn dress. She walked to the pond.
- The fish came up — and she stabbed it with a knife.
- That night the family ate the fish. Yexian did not know.
- She went to the pond. The water was empty.

**Vocab**: torn / dress / stab / knife / empty

### Beat 4 · The Old Man and the Bones
- An old man (老人) in a sky-colored robe came down to her.
- "Do not cry, child. The bones are under the dung heap."
- "Hide them. Speak to them. They will give you anything."
- Yexian dug. She found the bones. She kept them close.

**Vocab**: robe / cry / bone / dig / close

### Beat 5 · The Festival Night
- The 洞節 (cave festival) night came. Lanterns lit the village.
- Yexian whispered to the bones: "I want to go."
- A blue cloak (青衣) and small gold shoes (金履) appeared.
- She slipped out. The whole village stared as she passed.

**Vocab**: festival / lantern / cloak / shoe / pass

### Beat 6 · The Lost Shoe
- The step-mother's daughter pointed. "She looks like Yexian."
- Yexian ran. As she ran, one gold shoe fell off.
- She left it on the road. She came home with one shoe.
- The shoe travelled far. It reached the king of an island country, 陀汗 (Tuohan).

**Vocab**: point / fall off / road / one / reach

### Beat 7 · The King and the Foot
- The king tried the shoe on every woman. None fit.
- He came to the cave village and found Yexian.
- 鞋 (the shoe) slipped onto her foot like water.
- The king took her home. The step-mother and her daughter were buried under flying stones (飛石).

**Vocab**: try / fit / foot / take / stone

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (whose fish / whose shoe / who is at the door)
- B1+ vocab banned (no "stepmother" → "new wife" / "betrayed" / "summoned" / "treacherous")
- A2 replacements: stepmother → new wife / betrayed → fooled / summoned → called / treacherous → not safe
- Chinese (Hanzi) inline highlights are A2 cognitive aid, not lexical load — kept to 1-2 per sentence at most

## Story Voice

- Bilingual zh-en code-switch (per CLAUDE.md Ch7 spec)
- Primary EN narration; selected nouns get inline ZH (e.g. "the new wife, 後母")
- ZH is decoration, not test content — never put Hanzi inside option text or cloze blank
- Tang-era atmosphere kept: cave villages, festival, sky-colored robe, island king

## Theme

- 不是「灰姑娘穿鞋上位」是「失去媽媽留下的東西 → 媽媽以另一個形式回來幫她」
