# Canon — The Six Swans (六隻天鵝)

> Source: Grimm KHM 49, *Kinder- und Hausmärchen* 1812. Poetic dialogue-free narration.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch6.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Six Brothers, One Sister
- A king had seven children. Six were boys and one was a girl.
- They lived in a quiet castle near a wide green wood.
- The boys played by the river. The girl gathered flowers.
- The mother of the children was gone. The king missed her with all his heart.

**Vocab**: king / children / castle / wood / flower

### Beat 2 · The New Queen and Her Spell
- The king married a new woman from a far land.
- She did not love the seven children.
- One night, she made six small white shirts.
- She slipped into the boys' room and threw them on.

**Vocab**: marry / far / shirt / slip / throw

### Beat 3 · The Six Become Swans
- Where the boys had stood, six swans now rose.
- White wings beat. The window blew open.
- They flew into the moon's pale light.
- The little girl saw the empty beds in the morning.

**Vocab**: rose / wing / window / moon / empty

### Beat 4 · The Vow of Silence
- The sister found her brothers in a hut deep in the wood.
- They were swans by day, boys for one short hour at night.
- To free them, she had to sew six shirts from a thorn flower.
- She could not speak or laugh for six long years.

**Vocab**: find / hut / hour / sew / year

### Beat 5 · The King in the Forest
- A young king rode through the wood. He found her in a tree, silent.
- He brought her home and made her his bride.
- She still did not speak. She sewed and sewed.
- The young king's mother did not like the silent bride.

**Vocab**: young / tree / bride / sew / mother

### Beat 6 · The False Charge
- The bride had a child. The king's mother stole the baby.
- "She ate her own child," the mother told everyone.
- The bride could not speak to say it was a lie.
- It happened three times. The fire was built for her.

**Vocab**: stole / baby / lie / three times / fire

### Beat 7 · The Sky Turns White
- On the day of the fire, the six years ended at noon.
- Six swans came down from the sky. She threw the shirts on them.
- They became her brothers again — only the youngest still had one wing.
- She spoke at last. The truth was told. The fire went cold.

**Vocab**: noon / down / sky / wing / truth

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (time at night-window / who is the bride / why she does not speak)
- B1+ vocab banned (no "enchanted" / "wicked" / "execution" / "garments")
- A2 replacements: enchanted → put under a spell / wicked → bad / execution → fire (context) / garments → shirts

## Story Voice

- Poetic narration, no dialogue (per CLAUDE.md Ch6 spec)
- 3rd-person past tense throughout
- Imagery-heavy: "white wings beat" / "moon's pale light" / "sky turns white"
- The silence of the protagonist is mirrored in the lack of quoted speech in the text itself

## Theme

- 不是「壞繼母害人」是「為了愛的人沉默六年 → 真相終究會說出口」
