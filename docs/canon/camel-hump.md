# Canon — How the Camel Got His Hump (駱駝為什麼有駝峰)

> Source: Rudyard Kipling, *Just So Stories*, 1902. "O Best Beloved" 2nd-person narration.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch4.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The Lazy Camel
- Long ago, O Best Beloved, the world was new and shining.
- All the animals were learning to work for Man.
- But the Camel lived in the middle of the Howling Desert.
- He did no work at all. He only said one thing: "Humph!"

**Vocab**: long ago / world / new / work / desert

### Beat 2 · The Three Helpers Visit
- The Horse came to the Camel. "Come and trot like the rest of us."
- "Humph!" said the Camel.
- The Dog came. "Come and fetch and carry like the rest of us."
- "Humph!" said the Camel. And the Ox came too. Same answer.

**Vocab**: horse / dog / fetch / carry / answer

### Beat 3 · Man Sets a Rule
- The Horse and the Dog and the Ox went to Man.
- "The Camel will not work."
- Man said, "Then you three must work double — once for yourselves, once for him."
- The three were very tired. And very, very angry.

**Vocab**: rule / double / tired / angry / three

### Beat 4 · The Djinn Arrives
- A Djinn of All Deserts came rolling in a cloud of dust.
- The three animals told him their story.
- "Where is this Camel?" the Djinn asked.
- "In the middle of the Howling Desert, saying Humph."

**Vocab**: cloud / dust / story / ask / middle

### Beat 5 · The Warning
- The Djinn flew to the Camel. He looked at him in a long, slow way.
- "My friend," said the Djinn, "what is this I hear about no work?"
- "Humph!" said the Camel.
- The Djinn sat down. He put his chin on his hand. He began to make a Magic.

**Vocab**: friend / hear / sit down / chin / magic

### Beat 6 · The Hump Appears
- The Camel said "Humph!" one more time.
- And as he said it, his flat back began to puff up.
- It puffed and it puffed and it puffed.
- When the Camel looked behind him, he saw a great big lolloping hump.

**Vocab**: flat / back / puff / behind / hump

### Beat 7 · The Lesson
- "That is your very own Humph that you have brought on your very own self," said the Djinn.
- "Now you can work three days without eating, because you can live on your Humph."
- The Camel joined the three. He works to this day.
- And he still has his Humph, O Best Beloved, so he will never forget.

**Vocab**: own / bring / three days / live on / forget

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (who is tired / where Camel lives / why he gets hump)
- B1+ vocab banned (no "indignant" / "lolloping" beyond the noun / "exclaimed" / "scornful")
- A2 replacements: indignant → very angry / exclaimed → said loud / scornful → not nice
- Kipling's signature "O Best Beloved" preserved as proper noun (recognizable narrator tag, not lexical load)

## Story Voice

- 2nd-person narration to "O Best Beloved" — narrator speaks to the reader directly
- Slight oral-fable rhythm: "puffed and it puffed and it puffed" (Kipling triplets, A2-friendly)
- Each beat keeps a line addressed to the listener somewhere ("you" / "O Best Beloved")

## Theme

- 不是「懶惰被罰」是「逃避工作 → 反而背更重的東西」
- 對 user 的 hook: 「不想處理的事不會消失,只會累積成包袱」共鳴(下班逃 ≠ 永久逃)
