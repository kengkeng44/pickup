# Canon — The Tortoise and the Hare (龜兔賽跑)

> Source: Aesop's Fables, classical Greek oral tradition (~600 BCE), Perry Index #226.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch3 — dialogue-heavy voice.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The Boastful Hare
- The hare jumps fast through the field every day.
- "Look at me! I am the fastest!" he says to the other animals.
- The tortoise walks by, slow and quiet.
- "Hello, slow one," the hare laughs. "Can you even walk?"

**Vocab**: jump / fast / fastest / slow / laugh

### Beat 2 · Challenge — A Race Is Set
- The tortoise looks up. "I will race you," he says.
- The hare falls over laughing. "You? Race me?"
- "Yes. Tomorrow at the big tree."
- The fox agrees to be the judge. The animals all come to watch.

**Vocab**: race / tomorrow / tree / judge / watch

### Beat 3 · Start — The Hare Bolts Ahead
- "Ready, set, go!" calls the fox.
- The hare runs off. He is far ahead in one minute.
- The tortoise takes one step. Then another. Then another.
- "Poor slow thing," the hare thinks. "He cannot win."

**Vocab**: ready / run / minute / step / win

### Beat 4 · Nap — The Hare Stops to Rest
- The hare looks back. The tortoise is a tiny dot far away.
- "I have time. I will rest under this tree."
- He sits down. The sun is warm. His eyes close.
- "I will wake up in a moment," he says, and falls asleep.

**Vocab**: rest / sun / warm / close / sleep

### Beat 5 · Plod — The Tortoise Keeps Walking
- The tortoise walks past the sleeping hare.
- He does not stop. He does not look.
- His feet move one after the other, slow and sure.
- The other animals start to whisper. "Is the hare still sleeping?"

**Vocab**: past / stop / feet / sure / whisper

### Beat 6 · Wake — The Hare Sees the Finish
- The hare opens his eyes. The sun is low.
- He looks down the road. The tortoise is at the big tree.
- "No! No no no!" The hare jumps up and runs.
- His legs fly. The wind shakes his ears. But the tree is close to the tortoise, not him.

**Vocab**: open / road / fly / wind / close

### Beat 7 · Finish — Slow and Steady Wins
- The tortoise takes one last step. He touches the tree.
- The fox lifts a paw. "The tortoise wins!"
- The hare arrives, out of breath. He cannot speak.
- The tortoise smiles. "Slow and steady wins the race."

**Vocab**: last / touch / lift / breath / steady

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (sleep posture / who is ahead / time of day)
- B1+ vocab banned (no "boasted" / "smirked" / "trudged" / "victorious")
- A2 replacements: boasted → said loud / smirked → smiled / trudged → walked slow / victorious → the winner

## Story Voice

- Dialogue-heavy (per CLAUDE.md Ch3 spec)
- Past-and-present mix; speech tags simple ("he said" / "the fox calls")
- Each beat has at least 1 line of direct speech in quotes

## Theme

- 不是「勤勞 vs 懶惰」是「自信過頭 → 錯失」
- 對 user 的 hook: 「同事比較快不代表他贏」/「下班慢慢走也是一種路」共鳴
