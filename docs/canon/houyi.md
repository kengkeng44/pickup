# houyi — Canonical Story (后羿射日)

> Source: Chinese folk myth (中華神話民間口傳, 公有領域 public domain).
> Pickup Ch11 retelling, CEFR-A2 自創句式 (no specific translation cited).
> Pairs with Ch10 嫦娥奔月 (same myth cycle, different POV — Ch10 = Chang'e, Ch11 = Hou Yi).
> Theme: 「太多會燒壞世界 — 留下剛剛好的那一個」(too much will burn the world — keep only the one that is just right).
> Format: third-person narrator (奶奶 voice), past tense, ≤10 words per sentence.

---

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Ten Suns in the Sky

Long ago, the sky had one warm sun. The people grew rice. The rivers ran clear. Then one strange morning, ten suns came up at once. The grass turned brown. The rivers dried up. The children cried in the heat. The Jade Emperor in the sky saw the burning land.

**Key vocab**: sun / sky / morning / hot / dry / cry / land / ten

### Beat 2 · The Bow — Jade Emperor Calls Hou Yi

The Jade Emperor called a strong man named Hou Yi. Hou Yi was the best archer in the world. The Jade Emperor gave him a big red bow. He gave him ten white arrows. "Shoot down the nine extra suns," he said. "Keep only one for the people." Hou Yi held the bow tight. He looked at the burning sky.

**Key vocab**: bow / arrow / red / white / shoot / archer / strong / keep

### Beat 3 · The First Shot — Walking the Earth

Hou Yi walked across the dry land. He saw burning fields. He saw empty rivers. He lifted his big red bow. He pulled the string back slow. He took a deep breath. He let one arrow fly. One sun fell from the sky. The air felt a little cooler.

**Key vocab**: walk / field / lift / pull / string / breath / fly / fall

### Beat 4 · Nine Down — Will He Leave One?

Hou Yi shot the second sun. He shot the third. He shot more and more. One by one, the suns fell down. Soon nine suns were gone. Only one sun was left in the sky. Hou Yi lifted his bow one more time. He looked at the last sun.

**Key vocab**: second / third / one by one / soon / gone / left / last / more

### Beat 5 · Mercy for the Last Sun

Hou Yi did not shoot the last sun. He put the bow down. He let the last sun stay. The people came out of their homes. They saw warm light, not burning light. The rivers ran again. The grass turned green. The children laughed. The Jade Emperor in the sky watched it all.

**Key vocab**: put down / stay / home / light / warm / river / green / laugh

### Beat 6 · The King Turns Cold

The Jade Emperor was not happy. The nine suns were his own children. The king said, "You cannot be a god any more." Hou Yi gave up his place in the sky. He became a normal man. His wife Chang'e became a normal woman too. They went to live on the earth.

**Key vocab**: happy / own / god / give up / place / normal / wife / earth

### Beat 7 · Mortal Hou Yi and Mortal Chang'e

Hou Yi and Chang'e walked the green earth as people. They grew old like all people grow old. They were not gods, but they were free. Hou Yi kept his big red bow on the wall. He looked at it some nights. He looked at the one warm sun every morning. What came next was Chang'e's story.

**Key vocab**: old / free / wall / night / morning / next / story / kept

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: scorching / decimated / annihilated / slaughtered / vanquished / mercy (B1+). Use: hot / dry / fell / gave up / stopped.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No violence imagery (per user prompt + lint-cultural)**:
   - 'shot down the suns' OK — **never** 'killed the suns' (suns are not living)
   - 'gave up' OK — **never** 'sacrificed' / 'was punished cruelly'
   - 'the nine suns fell' OK — **never** 'died' / 'burned to death'
5. **Public-domain compliance**:
   - Story is Chinese folk myth, oral tradition. Public domain.
   - 自創 A2 句式 — do NOT echo wording from any specific translation (Birrell / Werner / Yang).
   - Names used: Hou Yi (后羿), Chang'e (嫦娥), Jade Emperor (玉皇大帝). All public-domain folk terms.
6. **Pairing with Ch10 嫦娥奔月**:
   - Ch11 is Hou Yi's POV (action, bow, mercy decision, mortal demotion)
   - Ch10 is Chang'e's POV (immortality elixir, flight to moon)
   - L7 ends pointing forward to Chang'e's story (open B6 hook — sets up Ch10 re-watch)
7. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
8. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Jade Emperor speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + warm)
- Past tense throughout (shot / fell / gave up / kept)

## Theme

- 不是「英雄打怪」是「**做完正確的事,即使要放下身份**」(do the right thing, even if you lose your status)
- 對 8-12 兒童 hook:「10 個太陽會怎樣?剩 1 個夠嗎?」物理直覺很強
