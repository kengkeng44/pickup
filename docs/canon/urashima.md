# urashima — Canonical Story (浦島太郎)

> Source: Japanese folk tale, classic oral tradition (古典口傳版).
> Public domain (oral folklore, no single author).
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch14.
> Theme: **時間是寶 — 救一條命、收一份善意, 但時間不會等你** (kindness rewarded but time waits for no one).
> Format: 海底奇遇 + 龍宮 1 天 = 人間 100 年 (Rip-Van-Winkle 母題東方版).

- 配 Ch1 桃太郎 — 兩個日本民間, 公有領域口傳, A2 自創句式不引特定譯本

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Urashima saves a turtle

Long ago, by a small village by the sea, lived a kind young fisherman. His name was Urashima Taro. One bright afternoon, he walked home from the beach. He saw three boys near the rocks. They were poking a small turtle with sticks. The turtle could not move. Urashima ran to the boys. He gave them some coins. He took the turtle to the sea. He set it gently on the wet sand.

**Key vocab**: fisherman / turtle / boys / sticks / coins / kind / beach / save

### Beat 2 · Down to the Sea — The turtle returns

A few days later, Urashima sat in his small boat. The sea was very still. A big turtle swam up beside him. "Thank you," said the turtle. "I am the one you saved. The sea king wants to meet you. Climb on my back." Urashima could not believe his ears. But he climbed on. The turtle dived down, deep into the blue water. Down and down they went, past bright fish and soft coral.

**Key vocab**: boat / still / climb / back / dive / deep / blue / coral

### Beat 3 · The Sea Palace — Princess welcomes him

At the bottom of the sea stood a great sea palace. The walls shone like pearl. The gates were made of shell. Inside, a beautiful princess sat on a throne. Her name was Otohime. "Welcome, Urashima," she said with a soft smile. "Thank you for saving me. I was that little turtle on the beach." She took his hand. She led him into a long dining hall.

**Key vocab**: palace / shine / pearl / shell / princess / throne / smile / dining hall

### Beat 4 · Happy days in the palace — But is he?

Urashima ate sweet fish and rice. He drank from a cup made of gold. Fish servants danced. Music played. Days passed like a dream. He laughed. He sang. He walked in coral gardens with the princess. Every meal was a feast. Every night was warm. He did not feel hungry. He did not feel cold. But sometimes, in the quiet hours, he thought of his old mother. He thought of his little house by the sea.

**Key vocab**: sweet / dance / music / dream / coral / feast / warm / mother

### Beat 5 · Asking to go home — The treasure box

One morning, Urashima told the princess: "I must go home. My mother is waiting. My village is waiting." The princess looked very sad. "If you must," she said. "I have a gift for you." She gave him a small red box. It was tied with a gold rope. "Take this Tamatebako," she said. "But please, please — never open it. Never. Promise me." Urashima held the box close. He promised. The big turtle was waiting at the gate.

**Key vocab**: home / waiting / sad / gift / red box / rope / promise / never

### Beat 6 · Back on land — No one knows him

The turtle brought him up to the surface. The beach looked the same. The sand was warm. But Urashima walked to his village and stopped. The houses were new. The trees were tall and strange. He saw an old, old man on a bench. "Excuse me," said Urashima. "Where is my mother's house?" The old man scratched his head. "Mother? Urashima Taro?" he said. "That name is in old stories. Long, long ago, a fisherman named Urashima rowed out and never came back. That was a hundred years ago."

**Key vocab**: surface / sand / village / new / tall / strange / bench / hundred years

### Beat 7 · Opening the box — A hundred years come back

Urashima sat down on the sand. His hands shook. His mother was gone. His friends were gone. His old life was gone. He looked at the small red box. He had promised. But he was alone. Slowly, he untied the gold rope. He opened the lid. A soft white cloud came out. The cloud rose up around him. When the wind cleared, Urashima was a very old man with a long beard. His hair was white as snow. The hundred years had come back to him at last.

**Key vocab**: shook / gone / alone / lid / cloud / rose / cleared / beard / white / hundred

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: enchanted / shimmered / glistened / palatial / vanished / lamented. Use: shone / nice / big / went / felt sad.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No death imagery**: 浦島 does NOT die. He becomes 'a very old man with a long beard'. Mother is 'gone' / 'in old stories', never 'dead' / 'died'.
5. **Sea palace = 'sea palace'**, sea king = 'sea king', princess Otohime = 'the princess' (drop name for A2 simplicity; Otohime only in beat 3 first intro).
6. **Time gap**: hundred years on land = one day in palace. Reinforced in beat 6 + 7.
7. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
8. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Princess + old man speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + warm + a little wistful at the end)
- Past tense throughout (saved / climbed / shone / promised / opened)
- Mochi + Hana 偶爾 react in outer frame (not inside main story)

## Theme

- 「救一條命會回報你; 但時間不會等你」(kindness comes back, but time keeps moving)
