# heungbu-nolbu — Canonical Story (興夫和孬夫)

> Source: Korean folk tale (흥부와 놀부), classic oral tradition (古典口傳版).
> Public domain (Korean oral folklore, no single author).
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch18.
> Theme: **善有善報, 惡有惡報 — 兄弟之間的選擇** (kindness rewarded, greed punished).
> Format: 兩兄弟 + 受傷小鳥 + 神奇種子 = 道德民間典型.

## Pairing & IP rationale

- 韓國民間口傳, 公有領域 (與 Ch14 浦島太郎 [日本] / Ch1 桃太郎 [日本] 構成東亞民間 trio)

- 葫蘆 = 'gourd' (A2 OK with picture context)
- 燕子 = 'swallow' 或 'small bird' (A2 兒童 friendly)
- **不寫死 / 不血腥** — 孬夫報應 = 'all his treasures turned to dust' (典型民間 transformation), 不是 'died' / 'killed'.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Two brothers, one kicked out

Long ago, there were two brothers. The older one was Nolbu. The younger one was Heungbu. Nolbu was greedy. Heungbu was kind. When their parents were gone, Nolbu took the big house. He took the rice. He took the cows. He kicked Heungbu out of the family home. Heungbu walked away with only one small bag.

**Key vocab**: brother / older / younger / greedy / kind / parents / gone / kicked out / bag

### Beat 2 · A poor home — How will Heungbu feed his family?

Heungbu had a wife. He had many small children. They lived in a tiny straw house. The rain came in. The wind blew through the wall. They were very poor. Heungbu worked all day. He cut grass. He carried water. He helped neighbours. But the rice was never enough. The children were always hungry.

**Key vocab**: wife / children / straw house / poor / work / grass / water / neighbours / hungry

### Beat 3 · A small bird falls — Will Heungbu save it?

One spring morning, Heungbu sat by his door. He looked up. A small swallow had built a nest on the roof. A baby bird was learning to fly. But it fell. Its little leg was hurt. The bird cried. A snake came near. Heungbu jumped up. He chased the snake away. He picked up the bird with soft hands.

**Key vocab**: spring / swallow / nest / roof / baby bird / fly / fell / leg / hurt / snake / soft

### Beat 4 · The bird returns in spring — A gift for Heungbu

Heungbu wrapped the bird's leg in cloth. He fed it small seeds. Soon the bird was strong. It flew away for the winter. When spring came again, the swallow came back. It dropped one small seed on Heungbu's hand. It was a gourd seed. Heungbu planted it. Big gourds grew on the vine.

**Key vocab**: wrap / cloth / fed / seeds / strong / flew / winter / spring / dropped / gourd / planted / vine

### Beat 5 · The gourds grow treasures — Heungbu becomes rich

Heungbu and his wife took down the biggest gourd. They cut it open with a saw. Out came rice. So much rice. They cut open the second gourd. Out came silver and gold. They cut open the third. Out came a new house. Out came warm clothes. Out came toys for the children. Heungbu was no longer poor. He thanked the swallow.

**Key vocab**: cut open / saw / rice / silver / gold / new house / clothes / toys / thanked

### Beat 6 · Nolbu copies — And hurts a bird

Nolbu heard the news. He was angry. "Why is my poor brother now rich?" he said. He went and asked Heungbu. Heungbu told him the whole story. Nolbu ran home. He climbed up to a swallow's nest. He grabbed a baby bird. He broke its leg on purpose. Then he wrapped it in cloth and pretended to help.

**Key vocab**: heard / angry / brother / asked / story / climbed / grabbed / broke / on purpose / pretended

### Beat 7 · Nolbu's gourds, Heungbu shares — What did Nolbu learn?

The next spring, the bird came back. It dropped a seed for Nolbu. Nolbu planted it. Big gourds grew. He cut them open one by one. But nothing good came out. Out came dust. Out came mud. All his treasures turned to dust. Nolbu was poor now. He went to his brother and cried. Heungbu opened the door. He shared his food. He shared his house. He shared his gold. "We are family," he said.

**Key vocab**: planted / cut / dust / mud / treasures / turned / poor / cried / shared / family

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: banished / inherited / lamented / wretched / cunning / malevolent. Use: kicked out / took / cried / poor / mean.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No death imagery**: parents are 'gone' / 'in old stories', never 'died' / 'dead'. Nolbu's punishment = 'all his treasures turned to dust', NOT 'killed' / 'burned'.
5. **Korean names**: 'Heungbu' (弟弟 善) + 'Nolbu' (哥哥 貪). Always spelled this way for A2 simplicity. First introduced in L1 + reused.
6. **Animal vocab**: 燕子 = 'swallow' or 'small bird' (interchangeable for A2). 葫蘆 = 'gourd' (with picture reinforcement in L4-L5 visuals).
7. **No blood / cruelty close-up**: snake 'came near' but Heungbu chases away. Nolbu hurting bird is shown but NOT graphic ('broke its leg on purpose' — sufficient + age-safe).
8. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
9. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Heungbu + Nolbu + bird speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + moral + warm at the end)
- Past tense throughout (kicked / saved / wrapped / dropped / shared)
- Mochi + Hana 偶爾 react in outer frame (not inside main story)

## Theme

- 「善有善報, 惡有惡報 — 但善良的人會原諒」(kindness comes back, greed brings ruin, but the kind forgive)
- 對 8-12 兒童 hook: 「同個媽生的兄弟, 一個善一個惡 — 你會選哪個?」(moral choice + family theme)
