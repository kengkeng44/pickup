# three-pigs — Canonical Story (三隻小豬)

> Source: Joseph Jacobs, *English Fairy Tales* (1890), public domain.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch8.
> Theme: **準備 + 努力勝過聰明取巧** (preparation + hard work beats clever shortcuts).
> Format: 對話體 + 三段式重複結構 (huff / puff / blow 兒童 verb 重複).

---

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Three Brothers Leave Home

Three little pigs grew up in their mother's house. One spring day, their mother said it was time. Time to leave. Time to build their own homes. The three brothers hugged her goodbye. They walked down the road together. The big bad wolf lived in the woods nearby. Mother said: "Be careful. Build a strong house."

**Key vocab**: pig / mother / leave / road / build / house / wolf / careful

### Beat 2 · Straw House — Fast and Easy

The first pig met a man with a cart full of straw. "Please give me some straw," said the first pig. "I want to build a house, fast." He took the straw and built a house in one hour. He sat down to rest. The wind blew. The straw walls swayed. But the first pig only smiled and ate his lunch.

**Key vocab**: straw / man / cart / fast / hour / rest / wind / wall

### Beat 3 · Wood House — A Little Better

The second pig met a man with a cart full of sticks. "Please give me some sticks," said the second pig. "I want a house too, but a little stronger." He took the sticks and built his house in one afternoon. He felt proud. The sun went down. From far away, on the path, came a soft sound. Heavy steps.

**Key vocab**: sticks / second / stronger / afternoon / proud / path / sound / steps

### Beat 4 · Wolf Arrives — Knock at the Straw Door

The third pig was still working. He carried bricks. He mixed wet mud. He built his walls high and thick. The other pigs laughed. "So slow!" they said. That same night, the wolf came to the straw house. He knocked on the door. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!" The first pig held his breath.

**Key vocab**: third / bricks / mud / thick / slow / knock / let / breath

### Beat 5 · Huff and Puff — Straw House Falls

"Not by the hair on my chin!" cried the first pig. The wolf smiled. "Then I'll huff, and I'll puff, and I'll blow your house in!" He huffed. He puffed. He blew with all his might. The straw house flew apart in seconds. The first pig ran fast. He ran to his brother's wood house. He banged on the door. "Let me in! Let me in!"

**Key vocab**: hair / chin / cried / huff / puff / blow / apart / banged

### Beat 6 · Wood House Falls — Run to Brick

The two pigs locked the wood door. The wolf came up the path. He knocked harder. "Little pigs, little pigs, let me come in!" "Not by the hair on our chins!" they cried. The wolf huffed. He puffed. He blew. The sticks broke. The roof fell. The pigs ran out the back. They ran fast to the brick house. The third pig opened the door just in time.

**Key vocab**: locked / harder / broke / roof / fell / back / brick / time

### Beat 7 · Brick House Wins — Wolf Gives Up

The wolf came to the brick house. He knocked. "Little pigs, let me in!" "Not by the hair on our chins!" The wolf huffed. He puffed. He blew, and blew, and blew. But the brick walls did not move. The wolf tried to climb the roof. The third pig built a hot fire in the pot. The wolf saw the smoke. He gave up. He ran back to the woods. The three pigs were safe inside, all warm by the fire.

**Key vocab**: brick / move / climb / roof / fire / pot / smoke / safe

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: demolish / collapsed / pursued / devoured / cunning / cowered. Use: fell / chased / ate / clever / shook.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No violence imagery**: 'tried to catch / chased' OK, **never** 'ate' / 'killed' the pigs (per feedback_pickup_listening_format + lint-cultural). Wolf gives up at the end.
5. **Three-fold repetition**: each "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!" / "Not by the hair on my chinny chin chin!" / "I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house in!" — kid mnemonic, **simplify to A2** ("hair on my chin" — drop "chinny chin chin" for A2).
6. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
7. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Wolf speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + warm)
- Past tense throughout (built / blew / ran / gave up)
- Mochi + Hana 偶爾 react in outer frame (not inside main story)
