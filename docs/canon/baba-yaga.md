# Canon — Baba Yaga (雞腳屋的老巫婆)

> Source: Russian folktale, oral tradition (collected by Afanasyev, 1855-1863). Dark folk-tale voice.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch5.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — A Girl with No Mother
- A girl named Vasilisa lived with her father in the forest's edge.
- Her mother was dead.
- A new woman came to the house. She did not like Vasilisa.
- The new woman gave her hard work every day. Vasilisa did not cry.

**Vocab**: girl / father / forest / dead / hard

### Beat 2 · The Errand — Go to Baba Yaga
- One night the fire in the house went out.
- "Go," said the new woman. "Go into the deep woods. Get fire from Baba Yaga."
- Vasilisa knew the name. Everyone knew the name.
- She took a small doll her mother left her. She walked into the trees.

**Vocab**: night / out / deep / woods / doll

### Beat 3 · The Woods — Three Riders
- The trees were tall and black. The path was thin.
- A white rider on a white horse passed her. Day came.
- A red rider on a red horse passed her. The sun stood high.
- A black rider on a black horse passed her. Night fell.

**Vocab**: tall / black / path / rider / sun

### Beat 4 · The House — Chicken Legs
- She saw a fence made of bones. Skulls sat on the posts. Their eyes glowed.
- Inside the fence was a small house.
- The house stood on two huge chicken legs.
- The legs turned. The door faced her.

**Vocab**: fence / bone / skull / glow / door

### Beat 5 · The Witch — Baba Yaga
- Baba Yaga came home in a great stone bowl. She pushed it with a long stick.
- Her nose was iron. Her teeth were long.
- "I smell a Russian girl," she said. "Why are you here?"
- "Grandmother, I came for fire."

**Vocab**: stone / bowl / push / stick / smell

### Beat 6 · The Tasks — Work or Be Eaten
- "Good," said Baba Yaga. "Do my work. Or I will eat you."
- She gave Vasilisa a great pile of rice mixed with black sand. Sort it by morning.
- The witch went to sleep. The skulls glowed in the dark.
- Vasilisa took out her little doll. "Help me," she whispered.

**Vocab**: pile / sort / morning / dark / whisper

### Beat 7 · The Skull — Light Going Home
- The doll worked all night. The rice was sorted at dawn.
- Baba Yaga was surprised. "Take your fire and go."
- She gave Vasilisa a skull with glowing eyes on a stick.
- Vasilisa carried it home through the woods. The new woman saw the light. She turned to ash.

**Vocab**: dawn / surprised / take / carry / ash

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (time of day via riders / who is the witch / what the doll does)
- B1+ vocab banned (no "stepmother" → "new woman" / "withered" / "ominous" / "cackled")
- A2 replacements: stepmother → new woman / withered → very old / ominous → bad-feeling / cackled → laughed loud

## Story Voice

- Sparse, plain, dark — short sentences, almost no adjectives
- Folk-tale present-and-past blend
- No internal monologue. Action and dialogue only.

## Theme

- 不是「壞繼母被燒」是「孤獨少女靠媽媽留下的小物熬過夜」
- 對 user 的 hook: 「最難的夜晚也有東西在你口袋裡撐你」共鳴
