# issun-boshi — Canonical Story (一寸法師)

> Source: Japanese folk tale, classic oral tradition (室町時代 origin, 御伽草子 13-16c).
> Public domain (oral folklore, no single author).
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch16.
> Theme: **小英雄也能打鬼 — 體型不是力量, 勇氣才是** (tiny size, big courage).
> Format: 拇指英雄歷險 (Thumbling motif) + 京都 visitor arc + demon defeat + magic mallet.

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Old couple longs for a child

Long ago, an old man and old woman lived by a rice field. They had no children. Every night they prayed for one. "Please, please give us a child," they said. "Even a tiny one will do." One spring morning, the old woman heard a small cry. There, in her hand, sat a baby — only as tall as her thumb. The old couple smiled and cried with joy.

**Key vocab**: old / man / woman / rice field / child / pray / tiny / thumb

### Beat 2 · A thumb-sized boy — What can he do?

They named him Issun-bōshi, which means "tiny boy." Years went by. He stayed thumb-sized. But he was clever and brave. He helped at home. He carried small things. He made his old mother laugh. One day, Issun said, "Father, Mother, I want to see Kyoto." His parents looked at each other. They were a little worried. But they nodded.

**Key vocab**: name / mean / clever / brave / help / carry / Kyoto / nod

### Beat 3 · A rice bowl boat — Off to Kyoto

His mother gave him a sewing needle for a sword. His father gave him a rice bowl for a boat. He used a chopstick as an oar. He climbed into the bowl and pushed off down the river. The water carried him far. He waved goodbye to his parents on the bank. The sky was big. The river was wide. Issun was very small — but he sat tall.

**Key vocab**: needle / sword / rice bowl / boat / chopstick / oar / river / wave

### Beat 4 · Guard in Kyoto — The princess walks out

After many days, Issun reached the great city. The houses were tall. The streets were busy. He walked to a lord's house and bowed. "Please let me serve you," he said in a small but clear voice. The lord laughed kindly and said yes. Issun became a guard for the lord's daughter, a young princess. One bright day, the princess walked out to the temple. Issun stood on her shoulder, watching everything.

**Key vocab**: city / street / lord / bow / serve / guard / daughter / princess / temple / shoulder

### Beat 5 · A demon comes — Can he stop it?

The path led through a quiet wood. Suddenly, a big red demon jumped from the trees. He had two horns and sharp teeth. "I will take the princess," the demon laughed. The princess held still, very scared. Issun jumped down to the ground. He pulled out his sewing-needle sword. "Stop!" he shouted. "I will not let you take her." The demon looked down and laughed even louder.

**Key vocab**: path / wood / red / demon / horn / sharp / scared / shout / stop

### Beat 6 · Inside the demon — Tiny but mighty

The demon picked up Issun and swallowed him whole. Inside, it was dark. But Issun was not scared. He held up his needle. He poked and poked the demon's tummy. The demon roared. "Ow! Ow! Ow!" he cried. He hopped from foot to foot. The princess covered her mouth in surprise. At last, the demon spit Issun out. He gave up and ran away into the wood. He dropped a small wooden mallet behind him.

**Key vocab**: pick up / swallow / dark / poke / tummy / roar / hop / spit / mallet / wood

### Beat 7 · The lucky mallet — Big enough now

The princess picked up the small mallet. "This is a lucky mallet," she said softly. "It can grant a wish." She tapped it once for Issun. With each tap, he grew taller. Tap. Tap. Tap. Soon he stood as tall as any young man. He looked at his hands and smiled. They walked back to the city together. The lord was so happy he hugged them both. Issun and the princess became family. He was big enough now to start a home.

**Key vocab**: lucky / mallet / wish / tap / grow / tall / hand / happy / family / home

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: dwarf / vanquished / banished / gallant / valiant / proclaimed / betrothed / matrimony / vanish / triumphant. Use: tiny / brave / smile / went home / big enough now.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No blood / death imagery**: demon does NOT die. He 'gave up and ran away'. The poking is silly + ouch, not violent.
5. **A2 + child-friendly word choices**:
   - 'tiny boy' / 'small boy' OK, **NOT** 'dwarf'
   - 'demon' (NOT 'oni' romanized, NOT 'devil')
   - 'a sewing needle for a sword' (A2 compound)
   - 'a rice bowl for a boat' (A2 compound)
   - 'big enough now' / 'they became family' (NOT 'married' — keep child-friendly)
   - 'started a home' / 'home together' (NOT 'wed' / 'marriage')
6. **Demon ending**: 'gave up and ran away' (no fight injury, no death)
7. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
8. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Princess + Issun + demon speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, warm + a little playful at the demon scene + tender at the end)
- Past tense throughout (lived / grew / built / picked up / spit out)
