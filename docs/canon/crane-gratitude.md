# crane-gratitude — Canonical Story (鶴的報恩, Tsuru no Ongaeshi)

> Source: Japanese folk tale, classic oral tradition (古典口傳版).
> Public domain (oral folklore, no single author).
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch17.
> Theme: **不能偷看的房間 — 善意有回報, 但好奇會打破信任** (kindness is returned, but curiosity breaks trust).
> Format: 報恩 + 禁忌 + 真相 = 經典「不可偷看」母題 (taboo-violation pattern).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The old man saves a crane

Long ago, in a small village in the snowy mountains, lived a kind old man. He lived alone in a tiny house. One cold morning, he walked through the snow to get firewood. He heard a soft cry. A beautiful white crane was caught in a hunter's trap. Its leg was hurt. The old man gently opened the trap. He freed the crane. He watched it fly up into the grey sky.

**Key vocab**: old man / snow / firewood / cry / crane / trap / hurt / free / fly

### Beat 2 · A young woman knocks — Who is she?

That same evening, the snow fell harder. The old man sat by his small fire. Suddenly, there was a soft knock at the door. He opened it. A beautiful young woman stood there. Snow covered her hair. "I am lost," she said. "May I stay tonight?" The old man let her in. He gave her hot soup. She smiled with a kind, quiet face.

**Key vocab**: evening / knock / door / young woman / lost / stay / soup / quiet / kind

### Beat 3 · She weaves a precious cloth — Why hide?

The next morning, the woman did not leave. She stayed and cooked and cleaned. She called the old man "Grandfather" with a soft voice. After many days, she said: "Grandfather, I want to weave you a cloth. But please — never look into the room while I weave. Never." The old man promised. She went into the small back room. The wooden loom began to click and clack all night long.

**Key vocab**: stay / cook / clean / weave / cloth / room / promise / loom / click / clack

### Beat 4 · The merchant praises the cloth — Curiosity grows

After three days, the woman came out. She held a soft white cloth. It shone like fresh snow. The old man took it to the market. A merchant saw it and gasped. "This is the finest cloth I have ever seen!" he said. He paid a big bag of gold. The old man went home very happy. But his heart asked: "How can she weave such magic? Who is she really?"

**Key vocab**: three days / soft / white / shine / market / merchant / gasp / finest / gold / magic

### Beat 5 · The urge to peek grows — Will he look?

The woman wove a second cloth, and then a third. Each one shone brighter than the last. The old man grew richer. But his curiosity grew too. Every night he sat by the door of the small back room. He heard the loom click and clack. He heard a strange, soft sound — like feathers brushing wood. "Just one peek," he thought. "Just one small peek. She will never know."

**Key vocab**: second / third / brighter / rich / curiosity / curiosity / loom / strange / soft / peek

### Beat 6 · He sees the crane pulling her feathers — Will she stay?

One cold midnight, the old man could not wait. He tiptoed to the door. He slid it open just a little. He looked inside. There was no young woman at the loom. There was a white crane. She was pulling her own feathers, one by one. She wove them into the cloth. Her body was thin and bare. The old man's heart broke. The crane turned her head. Their eyes met.

**Key vocab**: midnight / tiptoe / slide / look / crane / pulled / feathers / wove / thin / met

### Beat 7 · The crane flies away — What did he learn?

The next morning, the woman stood at the door. Tears shone in her eyes. "Grandfather," she said. "I was the crane you saved in the snow. I wanted to thank you. But now you have seen me. I cannot stay." She bowed. She walked to the door. As she stepped outside, she became a white crane again. She spread her wings. She flew up, up, up into the morning sky. The old man watched her go. He held the soft white cloth close to his heart. The sky was empty. But his heart was full of quiet sorrow — and quiet thanks.

**Key vocab**: tear / saved / thank / seen / cannot stay / bow / wing / fly / empty / quiet / sorrow / thanks

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid**: lamented / forsaken / yearned / enchanted / shimmered / vanished / forbade / cherished. Use: cried / left / wanted / shone / disappeared / told her not to / loved.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No death imagery**: 鶴 does NOT die. She 'flew away to the sky'. No 'killed' / 'died' / 'gave up' for the crane.
5. **No 'wife'**: per 兒童化規則, the young woman is **'companion'** OR (preferred for A2 + cultural softness) the old man calls her **'Grandfather'** style — i.e. she stays as **a young woman who lives with him as family**. Never marry / wife / husband lexis.
6. **Magic transformation phrasing**: 'female crane became a young woman' is A2 OK as oral-folk magic. Don't overdo metaphor.
7. **Feather-pulling**: 'pulled her own feathers, one by one' is A2 + non-blood. NEVER 'tore out' / 'ripped'.
8. **Hidden-room rule**: 'never look into the room' / 'never peek' — A2 simple, no archaic 'forbade thee enter'.
9. **Coherence**: all sentences within one lesson reflect same beat.
10. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Woman's speech tagged narrator too (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + warm + a little wistful at the end)
- Past tense throughout (saved / opened / promised / wove / flew)
- Mochi + Hana 偶爾 react in outer frame (not inside main story)
