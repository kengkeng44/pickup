# Canon — 司馬光砸缸 (Sima Guang Breaks the Jar)

> Source: Chinese folk legend, attached to historical figure Sima Guang (司馬光, 1019-1086 CE, Song dynasty historian). The 砸缸 anecdote first appears in the Song Shi (宋史, 14th c. Yuan dynasty official history) and Zhu Xi (朱熹, 12th c.) records — over 900 years of oral + recorded tradition. Public domain worldwide.
> **NOT used**: any modern Chinese textbook 譯本 / 動畫 / 繪本 post-1929 / picture book added phrasing. No specific named retelling's added details retained.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch23. Plot beats from the oral-and-official-history tradition only; A2 self-paraphrased sentences (no specific translation copied).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Children Play in the Garden
- Long ago in old China, a group of children played in a big garden.
- The sun was warm. The trees were tall. The children ran and laughed.
- Among them was a small boy named Sima Guang.
- He was quiet but he watched everything with bright eyes.

**Vocab**: garden / play / group / sun / boy

### Beat 2 · The Big Water Jar
- In one corner of the garden stood a very big water jar.
- It was taller than the children and full of water to the top.
- One boy looked up at the jar and wanted to see inside.
- He began to climb the side of the big water jar.

**Vocab**: corner / jar / tall / full / climb

### Beat 3 · The Boy Falls In
- The boy reached the top edge of the jar.
- For a moment he sat on the rim and smiled down at his friends.
- Then his foot slipped, and he fell into the water with a loud splash.
- The water closed over his head. He could not breathe.

**Vocab**: edge / slip / fall / water / splash

### Beat 4 · The Other Children Run for Help
- The other children stopped playing and stared with wide eyes.
- "Help! Help!" they cried, but no grown-up was close by.
- Most of them turned and ran out of the garden to find an adult.
- Their small feet ran fast on the path, but the gate was far away.

**Vocab**: stop / stare / cry / run / adult

### Beat 5 · Sima Guang Stays
- One small boy did not run with the others.
- Sima Guang stayed by the big jar, looking at the water.
- His friend was going under, and time was very short.
- "An adult will not come fast enough," he thought to himself.

**Vocab**: stay / look / short / time / think

### Beat 6 · He Picks Up a Stone
- Sima Guang looked around the garden for something hard.
- He picked up a heavy stone from the grass with both small hands.
- With all his strength, he threw the stone at the side of the jar.
- The jar cracked, then broke, and water poured out fast onto the ground.

**Vocab**: pick / stone / heavy / strength / break

### Beat 7 · The Friend Is Saved
- The water flowed out of the broken jar in a great rush.
- His friend came out with the water, coughing but alive.
- The other children came back with an adult, who saw what had happened.
- And people still tell this story today: in a hard moment, smart thinking can save a life.

**Vocab**: flow / come out / cough / alive / story
