# Canon — The Cowherd and the Weaver (牛郎織女)

> Source: Chinese folk oral tradition (Han dynasty origins, Qixi festival 七夕 anchor). Public domain — orally transmitted folk legend with no single author claim; canonical printed appearances date to *Classic of Poetry* (~1000 BCE) and Han-era *Yueling*. Used as the cultural origin of the 七夕 (Qixi / Double Seventh) festival.
> **Child-friendly retelling for Pickup Ch12** — A2 ELT pivot:
>   - Romance ('fall in love' / 'wife' / 'husband') → 'become close friends' / 'family'
>   - No death / kill words (Heavenly Queen takes weaver back, never harms)
>   - 'magic lady' (NOT 'fairy', avoid Western fairy mix-up)
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch12, paired with Qixi cultural anchor (一年只見一次的家人).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The Cowherd Meets the Magic Lady
- Long ago in China, a kind boy lived alone with one old cow.
- His name was Niulang (牛郎). He took the cow to the river every day.
- One bright day, a magic lady came down from the sky to bathe.
- Her name was Zhinu (織女). She wove cloth for the sky.

**Vocab**: cow / river / sky / lady / weave

### Beat 2 · Sewing Clothes Together
- Niulang and Zhinu sat by the river and talked all day.
- She showed him how to weave with bright silver thread.
- He showed her how to feed the old cow grass.
- They sewed warm clothes together for the long cold winter.

**Vocab**: thread / sew / clothes / cold / together

### Beat 3 · The Heavenly Queen Comes Down
- The Heavenly Queen (王母娘娘) watched from the high sky.
- Her face went hard. "Zhinu must come home and weave."
- One day she came down on a long white cloud.
- "Zhinu, go back to the sky. Your work is up there."

**Vocab**: queen / hard / cloud / back / work

### Beat 4 · The Silver River Splits Them
- The Queen pulled a silver pin from her hair.
- She drew a line across the sky from east to west.
- A great silver river (the Milky Way) flowed where she drew.
- Niulang stood on one side. Zhinu stood far on the other.

**Vocab**: pin / line / draw / flow / side

### Beat 5 · The Cow Helps Him Fly
- Niulang sat by the river and cried for many days.
- The old cow walked up slowly. "Take my soft skin," she said.
- "Wear it like a cloak. It will lift you up to the sky."
- Niulang put on the soft skin. His feet left the ground.

**Vocab**: cry / skin / wear / lift / ground

### Beat 6 · The Magpies Build a Bridge
- All the magpies (喜鵲) in the world flew up to the silver river.
- They held wing to wing. They made a long black bridge across.
- Niulang ran on the magpie bridge. Zhinu ran toward him.
- They met in the middle of the sky and held each other tight.

**Vocab**: magpie / wing / bridge / middle / tight

### Beat 7 · Once a Year on Qixi
- The Queen saw them meet. Her heart became a little soft.
- "Once each year," she said, "on the seventh night of the seventh moon."
- "The magpies will come. You may meet on the bridge for one night."
- And so every Qixi (七夕), Chinese families look at the sky and remember.

**Vocab**: year / moon / night / one / remember

## Public Domain Verification

- Source: Chinese folk oral tradition. Earliest references in *Classic of Poetry* (詩經, ~1000-600 BCE) and *Yueling* (月令, Han dynasty). No single author; orally transmitted for ~2,000+ years. Public domain in all jurisdictions worldwide.
- Cross-source: *Records of the Grand Historian* (史記, Sima Qian, ~94 BCE) references the Cowherd-Weaver constellations. *Jingchu Suishi Ji* (荊楚歲時記, Zong Lin, ~550 CE) documents the Qixi (七夕) festival.
- No modern adaptation elements retained:
  - No specific stage / film / TV character names
  - No 1950s+ Disneyfied romance plot
  - No specific translator's translation (Pickup retells in original A2 English)
- Cultural anchor: this is the origin myth of 七夕 (Qixi), Chinese Valentine's Day / Star Festival — explicitly NOT framed as romance for child audience; framed as family separation + once-a-year reunion (compatible with both child + adult readings).

## Theme

- 「七夕的鵲橋 — 一年只見一次的家人」
- 兒童框架: 不是「相愛分離」, 是「珍貴的家人被分開卻有一天能再見」
- 對小孩共鳴點: 想念家人 → 等待 → 重逢的喜悅 (爺爺奶奶 / 海外親戚 / 搬家朋友)
- 對家長 / 親子: 解釋七夕節由來,文化教育自然帶入
- 跨章節對應: Ch7 葉限 (媽媽留下的東西) + Ch9 灰姑娘 (善良內化) — 都是「失去 → 回來」框架, 牛郎織女是「失去 → 一年回來一次」變奏
