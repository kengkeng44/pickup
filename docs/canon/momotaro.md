# Momotaro 桃太郎 — Canonical Story

> Source: Japanese folk tale, classic oral tradition version (古典口傳版)
> Reference: Iwaya Sazanami's 1894 retelling + earlier Edo period versions

7 narrative beats, used as ground truth for Ch1 content authoring.

---

## Beat 1: Setup (老夫婦的安靜村莊)

Long ago, in a small mountain village in Japan, an old man and an old woman lived together. They were kind but they had no children. The old man went to the mountain every day to cut wood. The old woman went to the river every day to wash clothes.

**Key vocab**: old man / old woman / village / mountain / river / no children / wash clothes / cut wood

---

## Beat 2: Discovery (河邊巨桃)

One spring day, the old woman went to the river as always. As she washed clothes, she saw something pink and big floating on the water. It was a giant peach, as big as her head. She lifted the heavy peach and carried it home to share with the old man.

**Key vocab**: spring / pink / big / float / giant peach / head / lift / carry home

---

## Beat 3: Birth (桃子裡的男嬰)

The old couple put the peach on the table. They were about to cut it open when the peach split by itself. Inside was a healthy baby boy. The old couple were so happy. Because he came from a peach, they named him Momotaro, which means "peach boy."

**Key vocab**: table / cut open / split / baby boy / healthy / happy / name / peach boy

---

## Beat 4: Growth & Mission (桃太郎長大,鬼島消息)

Momotaro grew quickly. By the age of ten, he was strong and brave, and very kind. One day, news came: demons from Demon Island (Onigashima) were attacking villages. Momotaro told his parents: "I will go to Demon Island and defeat them."

**Key vocab**: grow / quickly / strong / brave / kind / news / demons / island / attack / defeat

---

## Beat 5: Departure (黍米糰子 + 三夥伴)

The old woman made special millet dumplings (kibi-dango) for Momotaro to take. On the road, he met a dog. "Give me a dumpling, and I will fight with you," said the dog. Momotaro shared. Next he met a monkey, then a pheasant. Each got a dumpling and joined him.

**Key vocab**: millet dumpling / road / dog / monkey / pheasant / share / join / fight with

---

## Beat 6: Battle (鬼島大戰)

Together they took a boat to Demon Island. The pheasant flew over the gate and pecked the demons. The monkey climbed in and opened the gate. The dog bit at the demons' legs. Momotaro fought the demon king with his sword. The demons fell to their knees and begged for mercy.

**Key vocab**: boat / gate / fly / peck / climb / bite / sword / fight / fall / beg for mercy

---

## Beat 7: Victory (凱旋回鄉)

The demons promised never to harm anyone again. They gave Momotaro all their treasures: gold, silver, and jewels. Momotaro and his three friends sailed home with the treasures. The whole village came out to welcome them. The old couple held Momotaro tight and cried with joy.

**Key vocab**: promise / treasure / gold / silver / jewel / sail home / welcome / hold tight / cry / joy

---

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words**
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR (no "garments", "volume", "fancier", "majestic")
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (no Mochi 1st-person inside main story — Ch0 already established outer frame)
4. **No contradictory test sentences**: listen-tf sentence must align with story (e.g. "The old couple had no children" — sentence matches, test is "Did they have children?" → No)
5. **Coherence**: all sentences within a lesson reflect same beat. No detail from beat 4 in beat 2.
