# change — Canonical Story (嫦娥奔月)

> Source: 中華神話民間口傳 (Chinese folk myth, public domain).
> Story architecture (后羿射日 → 西王母給藥 → 嫦娥吞藥飛月 → 玉兔陪伴) is public domain.
> A2 retelling — **自創句式**, 不複製 Disney 2020 / 教科書 / 任何特定現代繪本譯文.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch10.
> Theme: **為了保護心愛的人, 願意自己承受孤獨** (sacrifice for those you love — even if it means being alone).
> Format: narration 體 + 對話節制 + Mochi 不出聲 (奶奶 voice).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — A Hero and the Ten Suns

Long ago in old China, ten suns came up in the sky. The land was hot. The rivers dried up. Plants died. A strong young man, Hou Yi, lifted his bow. He shot the suns down, one by one. Only one sun was left. The land was saved. The people loved him. The Queen Mother said, "You may marry a kind girl." He married Chang'e.

**Key vocab**: sun / sky / hot / dry / young / bow / shoot / kind / marry

### Beat 2 · The Magic Pill from the Queen Mother

One spring day, the Queen Mother came down from her hill. She gave Hou Yi a small white pill. "This pill can let one live for ever," she said. "But only one of you can take it." Hou Yi looked at Chang'e. He did not want to leave her. He put the pill in a small box. He hid the box at home.

**Key vocab**: queen / hill / small / white / pill / live / forever / box / hide

### Beat 3 · A Bad Man Wants the Pill

Hou Yi had a student. The student was strong, but his heart was bad. He saw the white pill. He wanted to live for ever, too. One day, Hou Yi went out to hunt. The student stayed home. He pulled out a long sharp knife. He went to Chang'e's room. "Give me the pill!" he said. Chang'e stood very still.

**Key vocab**: student / heart / bad / hunt / stay / pull / sharp / knife / room

### Beat 4 · Chang'e Must Choose

Chang'e looked at the bad student. She looked at the pill in the box. If she gave it to him, he would live for ever and hurt many people. If she kept it, he would take it by force. There was only one way. She picked up the small white pill. She held it close to her mouth. The student ran at her.

**Key vocab**: bad / box / give / hurt / many / keep / take / force / way / pick

### Beat 5 · She Swallows the Pill

Chang'e put the pill in her mouth. She swallowed it down. Her feet left the floor. Her hair flew up. Her long dress lifted in the air. The student stopped. His mouth fell open. Chang'e began to float to the door, then to the window, then to the open sky. Her body felt very light. Her heart felt very sad.

**Key vocab**: swallow / feet / floor / hair / dress / lift / float / window / light

### Beat 6 · Flying to the Moon

Chang'e went up and up. She went past the trees. She went past the hills. She went past the clouds. The moon was big and bright that night. The moon felt closest to her. She flew to the moon. She landed on the cold grey ground. From up there, she could still see her home far below. Hou Yi was running back. He was too late.

**Key vocab**: past / cloud / moon / bright / night / closest / land / cold / grey

### Beat 7 · The Moon Rabbit and the Mid-Autumn Night

On the moon, Chang'e was not alone. A small white rabbit lived there. The rabbit was soft and warm. It sat next to her. It became her friend. Down on earth, Hou Yi looked up every night. He put out her favourite food in the yard. Once each year, in autumn, the moon is full and round. People look up. They see Chang'e and the rabbit. They miss the ones they love.

**Key vocab**: alone / rabbit / soft / warm / friend / favourite / autumn / full / round / miss

## A2 Authoring Constraints

When writing lesson questions based on these beats:

1. **Sentence length ≤ 10 English words** (per pickup-item-writer R1)
2. **Vocab level**: A2 CEFR. **Avoid B1+**: immortal / divine / sacrifice / ascend / banished / treacherous / apprentice / disciple. **Use A2 swap**: immortal → live for ever, divine → magic, sacrifice → give up, ascend → go up, banished → sent away, treacherous → not safe, apprentice/disciple → student.
3. **POV**: narrator 3rd-person past tense (奶奶說故事 voice)
4. **No violence imagery (anti-grim)**: 后羿射日 → 'the suns went down' / 'the suns went away' — **never** 'killed the suns'. Bad student threatens with knife but **never** stabs / wounds. Chang'e flies away, **never** dies. No blood, no death.
5. **Tang / pre-Tang atmosphere**: simple words for ancient China — 'long ago in old China', 'bow', 'pill', 'yard', 'hill'. No modern words.
6. **No specific modern adaptation**: no Disney 2020 *Over the Moon* characters (Fei Fei / Bungee / Gobi). No specific textbook phrasing. Self-authored A2 sentences only.
7. **Speaker tag**: predominantly narrator (奶奶 voice). Bad student dialog tagged narrator (dialog in quotes within narration).

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person narrator (奶奶's voice, calm + warm)
- Past tense throughout (came / shot / gave / hid / swallowed / flew / landed)
- Mochi + Hana 偶爾 react in outer frame (not inside main story)

## Theme

- 不是「嫦娥背叛后羿」也不是「嫦娥貪吃變仙」, 而是「**為了保護心愛的人, 願意自己承受孤獨**」
- 對 8-12 兒童 hook: 中秋節為什麼大家想念遠方的人? 嫦娥的故事給情緒一個名字
- 對家長 buy-in: classic 中華神話 + 中秋節文化資產 + 月亮上的兔子 (兒童最愛圖像)
