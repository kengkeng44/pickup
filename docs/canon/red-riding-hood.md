# Canon — Little Red Riding Hood (小紅帽)

> Source: Brothers Grimm, *Rotkäppchen* (KHM 26), *Kinder- und Hausmärchen* vol. 1 (Berlin: Realschulbuchhandlung, **1812 first edition**). Both Jacob (d.1863) and Wilhelm (d.1859) died well over 150 years ago — public domain in all jurisdictions worldwide (life + 70+ years).
> **NOT used**: Charles Perrault, *Le Petit Chaperon Rouge* (1697) — also public domain but story architecture differs (no rescue, wolf wins). Pickup uses Grimm 1812 because (a) the huntsman rescue beat is essential to A2 兒童 closure, (b) cross-source alignment with Ch9 灰姑娘 (Perrault but corroborated by Grimm 1812 KHM 21).
> **NOT used**: Any modern picture-book retelling, any Disney version, "Hoodwinked!" 2005 film, Sondheim *Into the Woods* (1986), Roald Dahl *Revolting Rhymes* (1982), or any other copyrighted derivative.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch13. Theme: **媽媽說別跟陌生人講話** (don't talk to strangers — first lesson on listening to grown-ups).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Mother's Warning
- Long ago in a village near the woods, a sweet little girl lived with her mother.
- The girl had a red hood that her grandma had made for her.
- People called her Little Red Riding Hood.
- One morning, her mother gave her a basket of cake and wine for sick grandma.

**Vocab**: red / hood / mother / village / wood

### Beat 2 · The Forest Path — Wolf Appears
- "Walk straight to grandma's house. Do not stop. Do not talk to anyone."
- "Stay on the path. The path is safe."
- Little Red Riding Hood went into the woods with the basket.
- A big bad wolf came out from behind a tree.

**Vocab**: path / safe / basket / tree / wolf

### Beat 3 · Wolf Asks the Way
- "Good morning, little girl," the wolf said in a soft voice.
- "Where are you going so early today?"
- The girl did not know wolves were bad. She told him everything.
- "I am going to grandma's house, down the path, by the three big oaks."

**Vocab**: morning / early / tell / house / oak

### Beat 4 · Wolf Runs Ahead
- The wolf had a quick plan. "Pick some flowers for grandma," he said.
- While the girl picked flowers, the wolf ran fast to grandma's house.
- He knocked on the door. "Open up. It is me, Little Red Riding Hood."
- Poor grandma was in bed, too sick to stand.

**Vocab**: plan / flower / run / fast / door

### Beat 5 · Wolf in Grandma's Bed
- The wolf took grandma into his stomach in one big bite.
- He put on her cap. He climbed into her bed.
- He pulled the blanket up to his nose.
- Little Red Riding Hood came in. The room was dark.

**Vocab**: cap / bed / blanket / nose / dark

### Beat 6 · The Huntsman Comes
- "Grandma, what big ears you have!" the girl said.
- "All the better to hear you with, my dear."
- "Grandma, what big teeth you have!" "All the better to eat you with!"
- The wolf jumped up and took the girl into his stomach too. Then he fell asleep.

**Vocab**: ear / teeth / hear / eat / sleep

### Beat 7 · Cut Open the Wolf
- A huntsman walked by the house. He heard loud snoring.
- He saw the big bad wolf in grandma's bed.
- He opened up the wolf and pulled them out. Grandma and the girl were safe.
- "I will always stay on the path," the girl said. "I will always listen to mother."

**Vocab**: huntsman / hear / open / pull / listen

## A2 Authoring Constraints

- Stem ≤ 11 words
- X3 anti-verbatim: correct option paraphrases the passage
- listen-tf must use inference (is the wolf kind / why did wolf ask the way / is grandma in the bed)
- B1+ vocab banned:
  - ❌ `devoured` → ✅ `kept` / `took into stomach`
  - ❌ `slumbered` → ✅ `fell asleep`
  - ❌ `cunning` → ✅ `bad` (kid-friendly direct)
  - ❌ `disguised` → ✅ `put on her cap` (concrete action)
- 兒童化處理 (A2 + 8-12 age safe surface):

- 'big bad wolf' OK — Grimm 原典正式名稱, A2 全 word
