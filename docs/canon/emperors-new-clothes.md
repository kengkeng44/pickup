# Canon — The Emperor's New Clothes (國王的新衣)

> Source: Hans Christian Andersen, *Kejserens nye Klæder* (1837, *Eventyr, fortalte for Børn. Første Samling. Tredie Hefte*). Public domain worldwide (Andersen died 1875, life + 70+; PD in US 1923+ rule for all 1837 texts).
> **NOT used**: any modern TV / animation / film adaptation. No specific named translator's later additions retained.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch15. Original 1837 plot beats only; A2 self-paraphrased sentences (no specific translation copied).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — The Vain Emperor
- Long ago, there lived an emperor who loved new clothes more than anything.
- He had a clean coat for every hour of the day.
- People said he cared more about his clothes than his country.
- One day, two strangers came to the city.

**Vocab**: emperor / clothes / new / clean / city

### Beat 2 · The Tricksters' Claim
- The two strangers said they could weave the finest cloth in the world.
- "This cloth has a secret rule," they said with a smile.
- "Only smart and good people can see it."
- "Anyone who is not smart enough will see only empty air."

**Vocab**: weave / cloth / secret / smart / empty

### Beat 3 · The Minister Looks
- The emperor sent his oldest minister to watch the work.
- The two strangers pointed at empty looms with proud hands.
- The old minister opened his eyes wide. He saw nothing at all.
- But he was afraid to say so. "Lovely! Truly lovely!" he said.

**Vocab**: minister / loom / point / afraid / lovely

### Beat 4 · The Emperor Looks
- At last the emperor himself came to see the cloth.
- He looked at the looms. He saw nothing.
- His heart went cold. "Am I not smart enough?" he thought.
- He smiled and said, "Oh, what fine colors! What soft cloth!"

**Vocab**: see / nothing / cold / fine / soft

### Beat 5 · The Parade Begins
- The strangers said the new clothes were ready for a big parade.
- They mimed putting the coat on the emperor with careful hands.
- The emperor stepped out wearing nothing at all.
- All the people came to watch. They all clapped and called out.

**Vocab**: ready / parade / step / watch / clap

### Beat 6 · The Child Speaks
- A small child stood at the front of the street.
- He looked at the emperor. He did not understand.
- "But he has no clothes on!" the child said in a clear voice.
- The street went quiet. Then everyone began to whisper.

**Vocab**: child / front / clear / quiet / whisper

### Beat 7 · The Emperor Walks On
- The emperor heard the child. His face turned red.
- He knew the truth now. Everyone knew.
- But he held his head high. He walked to the end of the parade.
- And the people watched him go, all the way to the palace gate.

**Vocab**: hear / truth / head / high / walk

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person past tense storytelling (grandma reading aloud to Mochi + Hana)
- Plain A2 English. No archaic vocab.
- No blood, no horror, no fear. The trick is intellectual not physical.
- Theme is **智取** (outwit by truth) — a small child speaks the simple truth.
- No Chinese (Hanzi) inside sentences / options / question prompts pre-reveal

## Theme

- 「為什麼大家都假裝看得到?」
- 大人因為怕被當「不夠聰明」而說謊;只有小孩沒包袱,看到什麼說什麼。
- 智取 + 誠實 + 同儕壓力 (peer pressure) — A2-friendly social lesson 對 8-12 兒童 + 親子家庭客群高度切題。
- 0 風險: 沒有血腥, 沒有壞人懲罰, 結局是國王繼續走完, 學到自尊比真相更難放下。
