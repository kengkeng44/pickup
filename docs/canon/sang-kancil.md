# Canon — Sang Kancil (鼠鹿 — 小動物用智慧過河)

> Source: Sang Kancil (Mouse Deer) oral folk tradition from Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and the broader Maritime Southeast Asia. Trickster animal cycle, pre-modern oral storytelling, **public domain in all jurisdictions worldwide**. No single named author. Functions in regional folklore as Anansi (West Africa) / Br'er Rabbit (Americas) / Reynard (Europe) do — the small, smart underdog who outwits larger animals.
> **NOT used**: any modern picture book retelling, any government-published Bahasa Malaysia / Indonesia textbook translation, any specific English children's edition. Pickup writes original A2 English prose summarising the public-domain plot beats only.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch19. Theme: **智取** (using your head, not your size).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Mouse Deer Sees the Fruit Tree
- Sang Kancil was a small mouse deer in the green forest.
- He was not big. He was not strong. But he was very smart.
- One morning, he stood by the side of a wide river.
- Across the water he saw a tall tree full of sweet ripe fruit.

**Vocab**: small / smart / river / fruit / tree

### Beat 2 · The Crocodiles Are in the River
- Sang Kancil wanted to eat that fruit so much.
- But the river was wide. He could not swim that far.
- Many crocodiles lived in the dark water of the river.
- Their long mouths waited under the river all day.

**Vocab**: wide / swim / crocodile / mouth / water

### Beat 3 · Mouse Deer Thinks of a Plan
- Sang Kancil sat down. He thought hard for a long time.
- Then a big smile came on his face. He had a plan.
- He walked to the side of the river. He called out loud.
- "Hello, crocodiles! I have a message from the king!"

**Vocab**: think / plan / smile / call / king

### Beat 4 · The Trick — A Message from the King
- The crocodiles came up from the water one by one.
- "What does the king want?" the big one asked.
- "The king wants to know how many crocodiles live here."
- "Line up across the river so I can count you all," he said.

**Vocab**: come up / count / line up / many / across

### Beat 5 · Jumping Across the Crocodile Backs
- The crocodiles lined up from this side to the other side.
- Sang Kancil jumped on the first crocodile back. "One!"
- He jumped to the next. "Two!" Then "Three!" "Four!"
- One by one, he hopped on every long crocodile back.

**Vocab**: jump / next / on / hop / back

### Beat 6 · The Crocodiles Find Out They Were Tricked
- Sang Kancil jumped off the last crocodile onto the other side.
- "Thank you, crocodiles! There is no message from the king!"
- The crocodiles opened their mouths in anger. They had no king.
- But Sang Kancil was already eating sweet fruit under the tree.

**Vocab**: off / other side / anger / already / sweet

### Beat 7 · Two Sides Learn Two Lessons
- Sang Kancil ate the fruit. He patted his small full belly.
- "A small body is fine. A smart head is better."
- The crocodiles slowly went back into the dark water.
- "We must not believe every voice from the side of the river."

**Vocab**: belly / fine / better / believe / voice
