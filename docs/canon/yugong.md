# Canon — Yu Gong Moves the Mountains (愚公移山)

> Source: 列子 (Liezi)《湯問》, classical Chinese parable, c. 春秋戰國 (Warring States). >2000 years old, public domain worldwide.
> **NOT used**: any modern picture book / TV / animation / film adaptation. No specific named translator's later additions retained.
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch25. Original Liezi plot beats only; A2 self-paraphrased sentences (no specific translation copied).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Two Big Mountains
- Long ago, there lived an old man called Yu Gong.
- His home stood between two very big mountains.
- The mountains made every trip out of his house very long.
- Every day Yu Gong had to walk around them to get to town.

**Vocab**: old man / home / mountains / very big / long walk

### Beat 2 · The Plan
- One evening, Yu Gong sat with his family by the door.
- "I want to move the mountains away from our home," he said.
- A neighbor heard him. The neighbor laughed and asked, "Is that even possible?"
- Yu Gong smiled and said, "Day by day, we can do it."

**Vocab**: family / move / neighbor / possible / day by day

### Beat 3 · Sons and Grandsons Help
- The next morning, his sons came out with baskets.
- His grandsons came too, with smaller baskets in small hands.
- They asked, "How should we carry all this stone?"
- Yu Gong said, "Take it to the river, one basket at a time."

**Vocab**: sons / grandsons / baskets / stone / river

### Beat 4 · Day by Day
- Every day they took one basket of stone and dirt away.
- The walk to the river was very long.
- A man passing by stopped to watch them.
- He laughed and said, "You are old. You cannot finish this."

**Vocab**: dirt / walk / river / passing / finish

### Beat 5 · The Smart Answer
- The man asked, "After you are gone, who will keep going?"
- Yu Gong put down his basket and stood up tall.
- He looked at his sons and his small grandsons.
- He smiled and gave a quiet, kind answer.

**Vocab**: after / gone / keep going / stand up / answer

### Beat 6 · Forever and Ever
- "I have sons. My sons have sons. My grandsons will have sons too."
- "Our family will go on forever and ever."
- "But the mountains cannot grow any bigger."
- "Day by day, we will move them away."

**Vocab**: forever / ever / family / grow / move away

### Beat 7 · The Kind Giants from Above
- Far above, kind giants from the sky were watching.
- They saw how the family worked together with strong hearts.
- They came down softly and carried the two mountains away.
- From that day, Yu Gong's home had a clear road out to the world.

**Vocab**: kind giants / sky / together / strong heart / clear road

## Story Voice

- 3rd-person past tense storytelling (grandma reading aloud to Mochi + Hana)
- Plain A2 English. No archaic vocab.
- No blood, no horror, no death scenes. The drama is intergenerational persistence.
- Theme is **堅持** (sticking with a small task over many days) + **家庭** (family across generations).
- No Chinese (Hanzi) inside sentences / options / question prompts pre-reveal

## Theme

- 「為什麼一件小事可以變成很大的事?」
- 堅持: 一筐一筐地搬, 看似不可能, 但日復一日就會成功。
- 家庭: 子子孫孫的力量加在一起, 比一個人強很多。
- 0 風險: 沒有血腥, 沒有死亡, 沒有壞人懲罰, 結局是天神幫忙搬走山, 家庭得到回報。
- 對 8-12 兒童 + 親子家庭客群高度切題 (爺爺奶奶、爸爸媽媽、小孩三代同心)。
