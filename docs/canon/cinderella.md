# Canon — Cinderella (灰姑娘)

> Source: Charles Perrault, *Cendrillon, ou la petite pantoufle de verre* (1697, Histoires ou Contes du temps passé). Public domain.
> Cross-check: Brothers Grimm, *Aschenputtel* (KHM 21, 1812). Public domain. Used for Beat 1 (father remarries) framing where Perrault is too sparse.
> **NOT used**: Walt Disney *Cinderella* (1950) — copyrighted. No glass slipper (Perrault original = `fur slipper` / pantoufle de vair OR gold-and-silver dress per Grimm), no Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo, no named Disney characters (no Lady Tremaine, Anastasia, Drizella, Lucifer, etc).
> CEFR-A2 retelling for Pickup Ch9, paired with Ch7 葉限 as the cross-cultural Cinderella pair (Tang Chinese root + French Perrault tradition).

## 7-Beat Arc (with key vocab)

### Beat 1 · Setup — Father Remarries
- Long ago in France, a kind girl lived with her father.
- Her own mother had died. Her father took a new wife.
- The new wife had two grown daughters of her own.
- They wore fine dresses. They had soft hands. They did no work.

**Vocab**: father / new / wife / daughter / fine

### Beat 2 · The Cinder Corner
- The new wife was not kind to the girl.
- She made the girl sweep, cook, and carry water all day.
- At night, the girl slept by the fire to stay warm.
- Her clothes got grey with ash. They called her Cinderella.

**Vocab**: sweep / cook / fire / ash / warm

### Beat 3 · The Ball Invitation
- The king's son was going to have a big ball.
- He wanted to find a wife. Every girl in town was asked.
- The two sisters bought new gowns. They laughed and danced.
- Cinderella helped them dress. But she could not go.

**Vocab**: king / ball / town / new / dress

### Beat 4 · The Fairy Godmother
- After the sisters left, Cinderella cried by the fire.
- A kind old woman in a soft blue cloak stepped from the dark.
- "I am your fairy godmother. Do not cry, child."
- "Bring me a pumpkin, six mice, and one big rat from the yard."

**Vocab**: cry / old / cloak / bring / rat

### Beat 5 · The Magic and the Warning
- The fairy godmother tapped the pumpkin. It became a gold coach.
- The mice became six white horses. The rat became the driver.
- Cinderella's grey rags turned into a long silver gown.
- "Come home by twelve," the fairy said. "The magic ends then."

**Vocab**: tap / gold / horse / silver / end

### Beat 6 · The Clock Strikes
- At the ball, the prince could not look away from her.
- They danced every dance. She forgot the time.
- The clock began to strike twelve. She ran for the door.
- One small fur slipper fell off on the stair.

**Vocab**: dance / clock / strike / run / fall

### Beat 7 · The Slipper Fits
- The prince held the slipper. He looked all over the country.
- He came to her house. The two sisters tried the slipper. It was too small.
- Cinderella came in. She set her foot in the slipper. It slid on like water.
- The prince knew her. He took her hand. She was kind, even to her sisters.

**Vocab**: hold / look / foot / hand / kind

## Public Domain Verification

- Source text: Charles Perrault, *Cendrillon, ou la petite pantoufle de verre*, in *Histoires ou Contes du temps passé* (Paris: Claude Barbin, 1697). Author died 1703 — public domain in all jurisdictions worldwide (life + 70+ years).
- Cross-source: Jacob & Wilhelm Grimm, *Aschenputtel* (KHM 21), *Kinder- und Hausmärchen* vol. 1 (1812). Both authors died before 1864 — public domain.
- No Disney elements retained: 
  - Disney 1950 character names (Lady Tremaine, Anastasia, Drizella, Jaq, Gus, Lucifer, Bruno, Fairy Godmother's specific design) — none used. 
  - Disney 1950 added scenes (mice making the dress, Lucifer the cat) — none used.
  - Disney coined phrases ("Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo", "A Dream Is a Wish Your Heart Makes") — none used.
- Slipper material: Perrault original = `pantoufle de vair` (fur slipper). "Glass slipper" entered tradition through later French editorial confusion (vair ≈ verre homophone) and was canonized by Disney. To stay Perrault-pure AND non-Disney we use `fur slipper`.

## Theme

- 「灰姑娘 — 不被看見也要善良」
- 葉限 (Ch7, 唐代) 跟 Cinderella (Ch9, 1697 French) 並列「跨文化灰姑娘」教學對
- 共通: 失去親媽 → 被新家庭壓 → 神秘外力 (魚靈 / 仙女) 給她一次站起來的機會 → 鞋讓世界看見她
- 不同: 葉限的力量來自媽媽留下的東西 (魚骨), Cinderella 來自外來的善心 (仙女教母); 葉限結尾後母被天罰, Cinderella 結尾原諒兩個姊姊 — Perrault 強調 `bonté` (善良) 是真正的魔法
