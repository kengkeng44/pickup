# 拾光 (Pickup) — Ch2-8 Narrative Arc

> **Total story arc**: Crybaby calico's journey home — but discovers home is where you're loved, not where you came from. **False-ending** = expected reunion with original family; **real ending** = chooses grandma's house as true home.

## Ch1 (recap, already shipped)

Rainy night. Cat wakes wet, cold, hungry, scared. Big shadow approaches — it's a kind woman (grandma) with a blue umbrella. Kind face. Cat is brought somewhere warm and safe.

**Words learned**: rain, fall, hard, cold, wet, fur, hungry, eaten, shadow, scared, big, kind, face, woman, dark, home, warm, safe.

---

## Ch2 — Grandma's House (Warm Welcome)

**Arc**: Grandma's home. Bowl of warm milk, a soft bed in the corner. The cat eats, drinks, sleeps deeply for the first time in days.

**Story beats**:
1. The house is warm and quiet. (vocab: house, warm, quiet)
2. She gives me a bowl of milk. (vocab: bowl, milk, give)
3. The milk is sweet. I drink it slowly. (vocab: sweet, drink, slowly)
4. She makes a soft bed for me. (vocab: make, soft, bed)
5. I lie down. My eyes feel heavy. (vocab: lie, eyes, heavy)
6. **Review**: house / bowl / soft / sleep

**Emotional beat**: From scared → grateful. Tears of relief, not fear.

---

## Ch3 — Morning (First Outing)

**Arc**: Sunrise. Cat wakes refreshed. Grandma opens the door. The cat steps outside — bright sun, fresh air, leaves on the road. Sees the world for the first time without fear.

**Story beats**:
1. The sun is up. I wake up early. (vocab: sun, up, early)
2. Grandma opens the door for me. (vocab: open, door, for)
3. The air smells fresh and new. (vocab: air, smell, fresh)
4. I see a green leaf on the road. (vocab: see, green, leaf, road)
5. I walk slowly. I am not afraid. (vocab: walk, afraid)
6. **Review**: sun / door / fresh / walk

**Emotional beat**: From relief → curiosity. Brave first steps.

---

## Ch4 — Shiba Friend (Companion)

**Arc**: A small shiba dog appears on the road. At first, cat is scared (memories of big shadow). But the dog wags its tail, drops a leaf at cat's feet — offering friendship. They play.

**Story beats**:
1. I hear a small sound. A dog comes near. (vocab: hear, sound, dog, near)
2. He looks at me. He does not bark. (vocab: look, bark)
3. He drops a leaf in front of me. (vocab: drop, front)
4. We play together in the sun. (vocab: play, together)
5. He is my new friend. I am happy. (vocab: new, friend, happy)
6. **Review**: dog / friend / play / together

**Emotional beat**: From cautious → joyful. First friendship.

---

## Ch5 — The River (First Challenge)

**Arc**: Cat and shiba walk together. They reach a river. Cat is afraid of water but must cross to continue. Shiba goes first, shows it's safe.

**Story beats**:
1. We walk for a long time. (vocab: walk, long, time)
2. A wide river is in front of us. (vocab: wide, river, in, front)
3. The water moves fast. I am afraid. (vocab: water, move, fast)
4. My friend walks first. He is brave. (vocab: first, brave)
5. I follow him. The water is cold but I cross. (vocab: follow, cross, but)
6. **Review**: river / brave / cross / follow

**Emotional beat**: From fear → courage. Crybaby cat still cries, but moves anyway.

---

## Ch6 — The Town (Lost & Lonely)

**Arc**: They enter a busy town. Loud noises, many people. Shiba is called away by his owner — cat is alone again. Lost in unfamiliar streets. Cries for the first time since Ch1.

**Story beats**:
1. The town is big and loud. (vocab: town, loud)
2. So many people walk fast. (vocab: many, people, fast)
3. My friend's owner calls him home. He must go. (vocab: owner, call, must)
4. I am alone. I do not know the way. (vocab: alone, know, way)
5. I cry a little. But I keep walking. (vocab: cry, little, keep)
6. **Review**: town / alone / cry / keep

**Emotional beat**: From happy → lonely. The crybaby returns, but the resilient side keeps moving.

---

## Ch7 — Memory (Old Home)

**Arc**: Cat smells something familiar — wet wood, old leaves. Memory: this is the alley where mother once was, before the rain night. But mother is gone. Only old smell remains.

**Story beats**:
1. I smell something I know. (vocab: smell, something, know)
2. This is the old alley. I remember it. (vocab: old, alley, remember)
3. My mother was here, long ago. (vocab: mother, here, long, ago)
4. But she is gone now. (vocab: gone, now)
5. I sit down. I feel small and quiet. (vocab: sit, feel, small)
6. **Review**: mother / remember / gone / quiet

**Emotional beat**: Bittersweet. Crybaby's tears are real, but accepting.

---

## Ch8 — The Choice (False-Ending → True Ending)

**Arc**: Cat stands at the alley. Could wait here for a family that may never come back. OR walk back to grandma's house — the warm bowl, soft bed, kind face. Cat chooses to return. The real home isn't where you came from; it's where you're loved.

**Story beats**:
1. I can stay here. Or I can go back. (vocab: stay, go, back)
2. The old place is empty. (vocab: place, empty)
3. But there is a warm bowl waiting. (vocab: wait, waiting)
4. Home is where I am loved. (vocab: where, love, loved) ← THESIS
5. I turn around and walk back. (vocab: turn, around, back)
6. **Review (final)**: choose / love / home / together

**Emotional beat**: Acceptance + agency. Crybaby cat is now choosing, not just reacting. The arc completes.
